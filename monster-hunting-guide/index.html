MONSTER HUNTING GUIDE

Kill monsters efficiently — maximize hunt rewards and event points without burning marches

Why Hunt Monsters?

Monster hunting is one of the most consistently rewarding daily activities in Puzzles & Conquest. While rallies and military expeditions grab most of the spotlight, a disciplined hunting routine quietly generates the gear materials, speedups, and silver that F2P players depend on to keep pace with spenders. If you are neglecting monster hunting, you are leaving your single largest free resource stream on the table.

What Monsters Drop

Every monster kill generates a loot package scaled to the monster's tier and type. The core reward categories are:

  • Gear Materials — The primary reason to hunt. Every gear set in the Blacksmith requires materials sourced from specific monster types. Without hunting, you cannot craft or upgrade meaningful gear.
  • Speedups — Construction and research speedups of varying duration. Higher-tier monsters drop longer speedups. Consistent daily hunting accumulates hours of speedups per week purely from kills.
  • Silver — Small amounts per kill, but it adds up meaningfully over a week of consistent hunting, especially on T3+ monsters.
  • Trap Materials — Resources used in Wall construction and trap upgrades drop from certain monster types, particularly Golems and Dark Knights.
  • Event Points — During Kingdom Monster Hunt events, every kill generates points toward milestone and leaderboard prizes. These prizes often include Epic-rarity materials, massive speedup bundles, and VIP points — rewards that dwarf the base kill drops.
  • Stamina Recovery Items — Some monsters and event chests reward Stamina Potions that can be stored and deployed during peak event windows.

The Dual Purpose of Hunting

Every active player should think of monster hunting as serving two simultaneous purposes that require different tactical approaches:

  1. Daily Farming Mode — Outside of events, dump your stamina each day on the highest-tier monster your march can kill cleanly in one or two hits. Prioritize the monster type whose material you currently need most in the Blacksmith. Consistency over weeks adds up to fully crafted gear sets that would otherwise take months.
  2. Event Mode — When a Kingdom Monster Hunt event is active, shift your priority to maximizing point generation. This may mean hunting a different monster type than your daily farm target, and it absolutely means reserving your Stamina Potions for the event window rather than burning them on off-days.
F2P Core Principle: Monster hunting is the backbone of the F2P gear pipeline. Paid players can buy material bundles from the store; F2P players earn them through disciplined daily hunting. A player who hunts every single day for 60 days will have materially more gear crafting capacity than someone who only hunts on event days. Consistency is the multiplier.

Hunting vs. Gathering: Which Matters More?

Both activities are important, but they feed different resource pools. Gathering fills your resource stockpile (food, wood, stone, gold) which powers construction and research queues. Hunting fills your Blacksmith material inventory which powers gear progression. The two are complementary, not competitive — a healthy daily routine incorporates both. The key distinction is that gathering can be done with any march at zero stamina cost, while hunting consumes stamina. Stamina is therefore your most precious hunting resource, and how you manage it determines how quickly your gear improves.

Quick Wins: Even if you only have 10 minutes a day for the game, spending your natural stamina regeneration on one or two monster kills every day creates a compounding gear advantage that is nearly impossible to replicate through other F2P means. Make it a daily habit before anything else.

Monster Types & Tiers

The kingdom map is populated with a variety of monster types, each associated with a specific gear material set and appearing in tiers 1 through 6. Understanding which monster drops which materials — and which tier you can efficiently kill — is the foundation of a smart hunting strategy.

Primary Monster Types

  • Dragons — The most commonly hunted monster type. Dragons drop War Set materials (weapons, armor, helmets). All five tiers are available in most kingdoms. Tier 3+ Dragons are the go-to daily farm monster for mid-game players building their first full War Set.
  • Dark Knights — Drop a mix of defense-focused gear materials and trap components. Dark Knights also contribute to Cavalry gear material drops at higher tiers. They are slightly tougher per tier than Dragons of equivalent tier.
  • Behemoths — Large, high-HP monsters that appear in T4–T6 variants. They drop high-rarity War Set and Infantry gear materials. Behemoths require strong hunt heroes and significant troop counts — not recommended for early-game players.
  • Golems — Rocky, slow monsters that drop Builder Set and Tech Set materials alongside trap components. If you are prioritizing infrastructure gear over combat gear, Golems are your primary target.
  • Serpents — Water or swamp-type monsters that drop Ranged gear and accessory materials. Serpents are particularly valuable if your main troop type is Ranged (Archers). Their spawn frequency is somewhat lower than Dragons in many kingdoms.
  • Wraiths — Undead-type monsters that appear at mid-to-high tiers. They drop a unique material pool used in certain Epic-tier accessories and relics. Wraiths have high Magic Resistance and benefit greatly from Hunt ATK research.
  • Chimeras — Mixed-type monsters that appear during specific kingdom events and elite monster spawns. They drop event-exclusive crafting materials and bonus event points. Chimeras do not appear on the standard map outside of events.
  • Alliance Dragons — A special category of monster tied entirely to the Alliance system. Alliance Dragons require coordinated multi-march attacks from alliance members and function differently from solo hunting. See the Alliance Monster Hunting section for full details.

Identifying Monsters on the Map

Monster icons on the kingdom map display their type and tier clearly. Tap a monster before attacking to see its current HP, which tells you whether your march has a realistic chance of killing it. Monsters that other players have already hit will display reduced HP — these partially-damaged monsters are often worth prioritizing since someone else has done the initial heavy lifting. Always check HP before spending stamina on a spawn.

Spawn Scouting: Monsters respawn on a timer after being killed. On event days, popular T4 and T5 spawn points fill with players competing for kills. Scouting the map edges and less-traveled kingdom zones during event windows often reveals full-HP monsters that other players have overlooked. A few minutes of map scouting before a hunt session can double your kill count.

Hunt Heroes

Monster hunting in Puzzles & Conquest is not just a troop-numbers game — it is fundamentally a hero game. Dedicated hunt heroes carry Hunt ATK and Hunt HP bonus stats that dramatically increase the damage output and survivability of your hunting march. The difference between sending the right five heroes versus your war heroes can mean the difference between a clean one-march kill and a failed attempt that costs you stamina with zero reward.

What Makes a Hunt Hero

Hunt heroes are defined by two key stat categories that appear in their skill trees and passive bonuses:

  • Hunt ATK (Hunt Attack) — Increases the total damage dealt per hunting march. Higher Hunt ATK means you can kill higher-tier monsters with fewer troops, and it's the primary reason dedicated hunt heroes outperform war heroes in the hunting field despite often having lower raw ATK stats.
  • Hunt HP (Hunt Health) — Reduces troop losses during hunting. While losing troops is generally minimal when hunting appropriately-tiered monsters, Hunt HP becomes important if you are pushing to the edge of your comfortable kill range or hunting T5–T6 monsters where incoming damage is significant.
  • Monster Damage % — Some hunt heroes have a specific bonus that increases damage against a particular monster type (e.g., Dragon Slayer bonuses). If you are primarily farming one monster type, a type-specific bonus hero can be extremely valuable.

Setting Up Your 5-Hero Hunt Squad

Every march supports five heroes, and all five contribute their passive bonuses to the hunt even if only the lead hero's active skill fires. Structure your squad as follows:

  1. Slot 1 (Lead) — Highest Hunt ATK hero: The lead hero's active skill fires in combat and their stats scale the strongest. Place your best Hunt ATK hero here. If you have a type-specific hunter (e.g., Dragon Slayer), and you are hunting that type, they belong in Slot 1.
  2. Slots 2–3 — Secondary Hunt ATK heroes: Fill with your next two strongest Hunt ATK heroes. Even if their bonuses are lower, stacking Hunt ATK across multiple heroes compounds total march damage significantly.
  3. Slots 4–5 — Hunt HP heroes or mixed utility: The remaining slots benefit from Hunt HP bonuses which reduce casualties. If you are hunting safely within your kill range, you may also use heroes with general March Capacity bonuses here to maximize troop count sent per march.

Hunt Hero Examples by Rarity

  • Epic Hunt Heroes — The top tier dedicated hunters with both Hunt ATK and specific monster-type bonuses. Unlock through specific campaign chapters or hunt events. These should be starred up aggressively if you plan to hunt T5+ content.
  • Rare Hunt Heroes — Available from summon scrolls and the Alliance Store. Provide solid Hunt ATK bonuses. A team of maxed Rare hunt heroes can comfortably farm T3–T4 monsters and are fully viable mid-game.
  • Common/Uncommon heroes with Hunt bonuses — Even low-rarity heroes with any Hunt ATK bonus are preferable to war heroes for hunting. Check your full hero roster for any Hunt bonuses — every passive bonus stacks.

Talent Sets for Hunt Heroes

Just as your combat heroes use the Conquest Talent Set, your hunt heroes should have their talents allocated toward the Hunt tree if your VIP level supports multiple talent presets. The Hunt talent tree nodes provide:

  • Additional Hunt ATK percentage bonuses
  • Stamina efficiency improvements
  • Increased monster loot drop rate
  • March HP for reduced casualty rates on hunts
CRITICAL WARNING — Never Use War Heroes for Hunting: This is one of the most common and damaging mistakes in Puzzles & Conquest. Your war heroes have high raw ATK, but zero Hunt ATK bonuses. Sending your lead war hero on a hunt march means you are effectively hunting without any of the hunt-specific multipliers. You will deal dramatically less damage per stamina point spent, require more marches per kill, and gain fewer materials per hunt session. Keep your war heroes on war duties — always. If you have not unlocked dedicated hunt heroes yet, prioritize doing so before spending stamina on T3+ monsters.
Hunt Hero Development Priority: Stars (star-up) matter more than hero level for hunt heroes. A 4-star Rare hunt hero outperforms a 1-star Epic hunt hero in most cases because star upgrades increase base stats multiplicatively. Focus your duplicate hero fragments on starring up your best hunt heroes before pushing levels. Level is secondary to stars in the hunt context.

Troop Configuration for Hunting

Sending the right hero squad is only half the equation. The troops you send, how many you send, and which type you deploy all affect both your kill reliability and your stamina efficiency. Optimizing your troop configuration is the difference between a clean, lossless kill and a failed march that returns empty-handed.

The "Full March, No Losses" Principle

The single most important troop configuration rule is: always send enough troops to kill the monster with zero or near-zero casualties, but never so many that casualties become significant. This principle drives every other troop decision:

  • Losing troops on monster hunts is a resource drain. Troops cost food and time to train. Losing T3 or T4 soldiers to a poorly-planned hunt march is a net negative even if you get the kill.
  • Sending too few troops and failing to kill the monster wastes the stamina cost with zero reward and leaves the monster at reduced HP for a competitor to claim.
  • The goal is the largest possible kill count per stamina point at zero troop loss.

Troop Count Guidelines by Monster Tier

These counts assume good hunt heroes (3+ star Rare or better) with moderate Hunt research. Adjust up if your heroes are weaker, or down if you have Epic heroes with heavy Hunt ATK research:

  • T1 Monsters: 2,000–3,000 troops. Any troop type works. Zero losses expected.
  • T2 Monsters: 5,000–8,000 troops. Any troop type. Minimal losses expected.
  • T3 Monsters: 12,000–20,000 troops. Single march kill. This is your F2P bread-and-butter tier.
  • T4 Monsters: 35,000–50,000 troops. Requires solid march capacity. Consider two marches if below 35k capacity.
  • T5 Monsters: 70,000–100,000 troops. Late-game territory. May require two marches even for strong players.
  • T6 Monsters: 120,000–200,000 troops. End-game only. Alliance coordination recommended.

Mixed Troops vs. Single Troop Type

Unlike PvP combat where troop composition relative to the enemy type matters enormously, monster hunting has no troop-type counter system. Monsters do not have Infantry/Cavalry/Ranged resistances in the same way that enemy lords do. However, there are still reasons to think about which troops you send:

  • Send your highest-tier troops — T3 troops deal more damage per unit than T2 troops regardless of type. Always send your highest available tier to maximize DPS per march slot.
  • Send your most expendable type — If your hunt march occasionally takes losses on the edge-of-range monsters, send the troop type you have the largest surplus of and can most easily replenish. This softens the resource cost of occasional casualties.
  • Mixed composition is fine — There is no mechanical downside to sending a mixed march (Infantry + Cavalry + Ranged) on hunts. The game does not penalize mix compositions the way some PvP scenarios do. Filling a mixed march is often the simplest approach.

Stamina Recovery Mechanics

Stamina is the resource that gates your hunt rate. Understanding how it regenerates helps you plan your daily routine:

  • Base Regeneration Rate: 1 stamina point every 6 minutes (10 per hour) at all castle levels. This is fixed and does not improve through building upgrades.
  • Maximum Stamina Cap: Base cap is 200 stamina. VIP levels can extend this cap significantly — VIP 6+ provides notable cap increases that allow you to bank stamina overnight without losing regenerated points to overflow.
  • VIP Stamina Bonuses: Higher VIP levels also provide a daily bonus stamina grant (VIP 4 and above). This bonus resets at midnight server time — claim it before the reset or it is lost.
  • Overflow Loss: If your stamina is at cap, regenerated points are wasted. Log in at least once every 12–14 hours to avoid overflow loss. If your cap is 200 and you regenerate 10/hour, you cap out in 20 hours from empty — daily logins prevent waste.
Stamina Planning: With a base 200 cap and 10/hour regeneration, and assuming 3 stamina per T3 kill, you can generate approximately 67 T3 monster kills from a full natural regeneration cycle (20 hours). That translates to roughly 67 loot packages of Uncommon/Rare materials per daily cycle from natural regen alone — before any Stamina Potion usage.

Monster Hunting Events

The Kingdom Monster Hunt event is the single highest-value event that dedicated hunters participate in. When active, it transforms your normal daily hunting routine into a scoreboard competition with massive milestone rewards and leaderboard prizes. Understanding how scoring works and how to strategically time your stamina use can double or triple the value you extract from the same number of monster kills.

How Kingdom Monster Hunt Works

The Kingdom Monster Hunt typically runs for 24 hours (sometimes 48 hours during special editions) and appears on a regular rotation in the event calendar. During the event window:

  • Every monster kill generates Hunt Points scaled to the monster's tier. A T5 monster kill generates significantly more points than a T3 kill of the same stamina cost.
  • Points accumulate toward both personal milestones (guaranteed rewards at point thresholds) and a kingdom leaderboard (top-ranking players receive bonus prize tiers).
  • All kingdoms compete simultaneously — there is no cross-kingdom competition. You are competing against other players in your own kingdom for leaderboard positions.
  • Alliance monster kills (Alliance Dragon) may or may not count toward hunt event points depending on the specific event rules — always check the event description before assuming.

Milestone Reward Tiers

Personal milestones are the guaranteed prize tier that every participating player can reach regardless of leaderboard position. Typical milestone thresholds and their reward quality:

  • Milestone 1 (~5,000 pts) — Common/Uncommon gear material bundles, small speedup packs. Accessible to all players who hunt at all during the event.
  • Milestone 2 (~25,000 pts) — Rare gear material bundles, medium speedup packs, some VIP points. Requires focused hunting — roughly 50 T3 kills or 12–13 T4 kills.
  • Milestone 3 (~80,000 pts) — Epic material fragments, large speedup bundles, Stamina Potions. Requires heavy Stamina Potion usage or T4/T5 capability.
  • Milestone 4 (~200,000 pts) — Epic material sets, significant speedup bundles, hero XP items, premium currency fragments. Late-game target requiring T5 farming or mass potion use.
  • Milestone 5+ (~500,000 pts) — Full Epic gear sets, large premium bundles. Primarily for end-game players with T6 access and large potion stockpiles.

Leaderboard Prize Strategy

Leaderboard positions 1–3, 4–10, and 11–30 typically each receive their own prize tier with the top 3 receiving the most valuable rewards. For F2P players, chasing top leaderboard positions is generally not cost-effective — the top ranks are dominated by heavy spenders who can buy unlimited Stamina Potions. Instead, focus your strategy on:

  1. Reaching Milestone 2 every event as a minimum
  2. Reaching Milestone 3 on events where you have saved Stamina Potions
  3. Making a leaderboard push only if the top-30 position looks achievable in your kingdom given current competition
Event Stamina Strategy: Check the leaderboard mid-event before spending your Stamina Potions. If the top-30 threshold is achievable, deploy your potions in the final 4–6 hours when other players have exhausted their resources. Last-minute surges are a legitimate leaderboard strategy that many players overlook.

Gear Materials from Monsters

Each monster type in Puzzles & Conquest drops materials linked to a specific gear set. Matching your daily hunting target to your current Blacksmith priority is the most efficient use of your stamina outside of events. Randomly hunting whatever appears on the map generates materials you may not need while starving your current crafting queue.

Dark Knights Defense gear, trap components Cavalry gear fragments Upgrading walls / Cavalry focus
Behemoths High-tier War Set, Infantry gear Rare+ speedups Epic War Set upgrade push
Golems Builder Set, Tech Set materials Trap components, stone Infrastructure gear (Builder/Tech)

Material Rarity and Monster Tier

Within any monster type, the tier of monster you kill directly determines the rarity of materials dropped:

  • T1–T2 Monsters → Common and Uncommon materials. Only useful for gear you are crafting as a temporary placeholder. Do not stockpile these.
  • T3 Monsters → Primarily Uncommon with a small Rare drop chance. The consistent daily farm tier for mid-game F2P players working toward Rare gear sets.
  • T4 Monsters → Reliable Rare drops with increasing Epic fragment rates. The target tier once you can kill cleanly. Epic fragments from T4 kills accumulate into full Epic materials over time.
  • T5 Monsters → Guaranteed Rare, frequent Epic full-material drops. The gear progression accelerates dramatically here. Late-game players farming T5 Dragons nightly can complete an Epic War Set piece within 2–3 weeks of consistent hunting.
  • T6 Monsters → Epic materials guaranteed per kill, with chance at Legendary fragments in some special events. End-game only.

Blacksmith-Driven Hunting Priority

The most efficient hunters work backward from their Blacksmith queue. Before each hunting session, check what your next craft requires and target the appropriate monster type. The priority order for most F2P players follows a simple logic:

  1. First Priority — War Set completion: Hunt Dragons until your full Rare War Set is complete. This unlocks meaningful combat power improvement and is the baseline for Military Expedition progress.
  2. Second Priority — War Set to Epic: Continue Dragon hunting (upgrade to T4 targets) to accumulate Epic War Set materials. Start with the Weapon slot as it provides the largest single combat stat increase.
  3. Third Priority — Builder / Tech Set: Shift to Golems once your War Set is solidly Rare. Builder and Tech Sets have significant long-term value even at Rare tier, and Epic Builder Set pieces save enormous construction time.
  4. Fourth Priority — Specialty gear: Serpents for Ranged gear, Dark Knights for Cavalry-focused builds, Wraiths for accessory upgrades. These are secondary to the War Set pipeline for most players.
Material Hoarding Alert: Do not stockpile materials "for later." The Blacksmith has no material expiry, but your gear progression actively suffers while you wait. Craft the moment you have enough materials for the next tier. An equipped Rare piece provides real combat power; materials sitting in your inventory provide none.

Alliance Monster Hunting

Separate from solo monster hunting, the Alliance Dragon system is a coordinated group activity where alliance members collectively attack shared, high-HP dragon monsters that no single player can kill alone. Alliance Dragon hunting operates on its own rules, rewards, and coordination requirements.

Alliance Dragon Overview

Alliance Dragons spawn in the kingdom at specific times set by the Alliance Leader or R4 officers. They have dramatically more HP than standard solo dragons of the same visual tier and are designed to require 10–30 or more coordinated march attacks to defeat. When the dragon is killed, all participating members receive rewards scaled to their individual contribution damage.

How Contribution Works

Your share of the Alliance Dragon reward chest is determined by your contribution score — the total damage your marches dealt to the Alliance Dragon. This means:

  • Players with stronger hunt heroes and more troops get larger reward shares
  • The total chest rewards are fixed regardless of how many players participated, so fewer participants = larger individual shares
  • Players who arrive late (after the dragon is nearly dead) still receive some reward, but a smaller share than those who contributed from the start
  • There is no stamina cost for Alliance Dragon attacks — they consume a separate "Alliance Stamina" resource that regenerates separately from your personal monster stamina

Alliance Dragon Reward Types

Alliance Dragon chests contain rewards distinct from solo monster drops:

  • Alliance Credits — The primary alliance currency. Used in the Alliance Store for exclusive items not available elsewhere including gear materials, speedups, and hero fragments.
  • Alliance Chest Points — Contribution toward the Alliance Chest milestone. When the alliance collectively accumulates enough chest points, an alliance-wide chest opens distributing bonus rewards to all members simultaneously.
  • Exclusive Hunt Materials — Some Alliance Dragon tiers drop materials used exclusively in alliance-tier gear recipes that solo hunting cannot provide.
  • VIP Points — Higher-tier Alliance Dragons contribute VIP points to all participants, providing long-term VIP level progression for F2P players.

Coordinating Alliance Dragon Attacks

Effective Alliance Dragon hunting requires coordination that goes beyond simply tapping the dragon when you see it. Best practices:

  1. Wait for the signal: Alliance officers typically coordinate attacks via alliance chat or an external Discord server. Hitting the dragon before the alliance is ready can waste the spawn if not enough members are online to finish the kill.
  2. Stack marches simultaneously: Multiple marches hitting the dragon simultaneously (within the same round) deal dramatically more total damage than sequential individual attacks. Use the rally system if your alliance is organized enough to coordinate rally timing.
  3. Always send your best hunt hero: Even in Alliance Dragon mode, your Hunt ATK bonus applies to your individual march's damage contribution. Sending war heroes reduces your contribution share.
  4. Participate every spawn: Alliance Dragon chests compound — consistent participation across all spawns accumulates Alliance Credits far faster than occasional bursts. Even a small march is worth sending.

Alliance Dragon Tiers

Alliance Dragons appear in four or more tiers with progressively more HP and significantly better rewards. The alliance's collective march power determines which tier of dragon you can reliably kill. Most active mid-game alliances can handle T2 and T3 Alliance Dragons. Late-game alliances in developed kingdoms may target T5 Alliance Dragons that provide the best exclusive material drops.

Alliance Selection Tip: When choosing or evaluating an alliance, check how often they kill Alliance Dragons and at what tier. An active alliance killing T3+ Alliance Dragons daily provides more material value to members than a larger but inactive alliance that rarely completes kills. Dragon kill rate is a direct indicator of alliance activity and coordination quality.

Stamina Management

Stamina is the single limiting resource of monster hunting. Every stamina point you waste through poor management — overflow loss, spending it outside event windows, or inefficient use on low-tier monsters — directly subtracts from your gear progression. Disciplined stamina management is what separates efficient hunters from players who feel like they are always behind on gear crafting.

Stamina Economy Fundamentals

  • Base Regen Rate: 10 stamina per hour (1 per 6 minutes). This rate is constant regardless of castle level, VIP level, or any building upgrade. It cannot be increased through research or construction.
  • Daily Natural Stamina: 240 stamina per day from base regen alone (10/hr × 24 hrs). Enough for 80 T3 kills or 48 T4 kills purely from natural regen with no cap overflow.
  • Base Cap: 200 stamina. At base cap, overflow loss begins after ~20 hours from empty. Daily logins are sufficient to prevent overflow losses at base cap.
  • VIP Extended Cap: VIP 4 extends cap to ~240, VIP 8 to ~300+. Higher cap means you can sleep longer or skip a login without losing regen. Extended cap is one of the most underrated benefits of VIP progression for F2P players who prioritize hunting.

VIP Stamina Bonuses by Level

VIP Level Max Stamina Cap Daily Bonus Stamina Notes
VIP 0–3 200 0 Base. Login once daily to avoid overflow.
VIP 4–5 240 +20/day First meaningful tier. Daily bonus worth claiming.
VIP 6–7 280 +40/day Solid stamina economy. Can miss one login.
VIP 8–9 320 +60/day Strong bonus. Extended cap reduces overflow stress.
VIP 10+ 400+ +100+/day Top tier stamina economy. Farm T5+ reliably every day.

Stamina Items and Sources

Beyond natural regeneration and VIP bonuses, stamina can be replenished through items:

  • Stamina Potions (Small / Medium / Large) — The primary stockpile resource for event-day bursts. Small gives +20 stamina, Medium +50, Large +100. Hoard these relentlessly outside of events.
  • Event Milestone Rewards — Hunt events and other event types frequently award Stamina Potions as milestone prizes, creating a positive feedback loop for consistent hunters.
  • Alliance Store — Stamina items appear occasionally in the Alliance Store for Alliance Credits. When they appear, buying them is almost always worth it if you have the credits.
  • Daily Login Rewards — Some login streak milestones include stamina bundles. Never miss a daily login during streak bonus periods.
  • Kingdom / Alliance Events — Various non-hunt events award stamina as participation prizes. Even if you don't hunt during those events, collect the stamina awards and stockpile them.

Daily Stamina Routine for F2P Players

A sustainable F2P stamina routine that prevents overflow and maximizes material generation:

  1. Morning login (6–8 AM): Spend all available stamina on your target monster. Collect any daily VIP bonus. Aim to bring stamina to zero or near-zero before closing the app.
  2. Midday check (12–2 PM): You have regenerated ~60–80 stamina (6–8 hours × 10/hr). Quick hunt session of 15–20 kills on your target monster, then close. This prevents cap overflow for players with base 200 cap.
  3. Evening session (7–10 PM): Main hunt session. Spend all regenerated stamina + any saved potions if an event is active. This is your primary farming window.
  4. Pre-sleep dump: If you tend to sleep 8+ hours, spend stamina before bed to below 50 if your cap is 200. You will regenerate ~80 while sleeping, coming back to ~130 — well below cap.
Event Week Adjustment: In the week containing a Kingdom Monster Hunt event, shift your Stamina Potion usage to zero until the event goes live. Stop using potions 3 days before the event. Stack your natural regen into your cap. Then deploy everything — natural regen + saved potions — during the event window for maximum event points.

Monster Hunting Research

The Academy's Military research tree contains a dedicated branch for monster hunting that provides permanent, compounding bonuses to your entire hunting operation. Hunt research is one of the most impactful long-term investments a hunting-focused player can make, yet it is frequently underprioritized relative to combat research. Understanding which nodes matter and in what order to unlock them is essential for building a sustainable hunting operation.

Key Research Categories for Hunters

  • Hunt ATK — Increases the total damage all your hunt marches deal against monsters. This is the highest priority hunt research node. Even modest Hunt ATK percentages compound across thousands of monthly kills to meaningfully reduce the troop count required per kill tier.
  • Hunt HP — Increases the effective HP of your hunt march, directly reducing troop casualties. For players pushing the upper edge of their kill range (e.g., T4 kills with T3-capable marches), Hunt HP research can be the difference between zero casualties and significant troop losses per kill.
  • Monster Drop Rate — Increases the quantity and rarity of materials dropped per monster kill. This is arguably the single most impactful research for material farming efficiency. A 30% drop rate bonus means 30% more materials from the same stamina investment — the effect compounds directly into faster gear progression.
  • Stamina Recovery Speed — Reduces the time between stamina points regenerating. Even small improvements to stamina recovery meaningfully increase your daily kill ceiling over time. Note: this may appear in the VIP research tree rather than the Military tree depending on your game version.
  • March Capacity (Hunt) — Some research trees contain hunt-specific march capacity bonuses that increase the maximum troop count in hunt marches independently of your war march capacity. These are useful when scaling to higher monster tiers that require larger troop counts.

Research Priority Order for Dedicated Hunters

  1. Monster Drop Rate (highest priority) — Complete every available tier of drop rate research before focusing on ATK or HP nodes. Drop rate affects every single kill you will ever make — the ROI is permanent and compounding.
  2. Hunt ATK Tier 1–3 — Unlock enough Hunt ATK to clean-kill your current target monster tier with significantly fewer troops than base. This reduces march setup time and enables faster hunt sessions.
  3. Hunt HP Tier 1–2 — Reach zero-casualty status on your current target tier. Casualties slow your army growth and create hidden resource costs that undermine hunting efficiency.
  4. Hunt ATK Tier 4+ — Push to the next monster tier threshold. Higher Hunt ATK is the enabler for moving from T3 to T4, T4 to T5 kill capability.
  5. Stamina Recovery — Unlock once the above foundational nodes are complete. The stamina benefit is real but secondary to kill power and drop efficiency.

Research Tree Navigation Tips

The hunt research nodes are typically deeper in the Military research tree and require completing prerequisite combat research nodes to unlock. This creates an important planning consideration:

  • You cannot rush straight to hunt research at low Academy levels. Combat research prerequisites must be completed first, which has the positive side effect of improving your general combat power simultaneously.
  • Use the Academy's research planning view to map out the path to your target hunt nodes before committing resources. Some nodes have multiple prerequisite chains that are not immediately obvious.
  • Tech Set gear (crafted from Golem materials) reduces research time when worn during Academy queuing. If you are pushing a major hunt research chain, consider queuing from your Builder/Tech Set hero first.
Alliance Research Bonus: Many alliances unlock alliance research buffs that provide a flat percentage bonus to all hunt stats (ATK, HP, drop rate) for all alliance members simultaneously. These buffs stack on top of your personal research. Always be in an alliance with active research officers — the hunt bonuses alone can be worth 15–25% effective stat improvement over your personal research numbers.
Research Speedup Allocation: Monster hunting research is not the highest-priority research category for most players (Combat and Construction research take precedence for overall power). However, for players who are at a comfortable plateau in combat development and want to optimize their passive material income, shifting a portion of weekly speedup allocation to hunt research nodes is highly effective.

F2P Monster Hunting Strategy

Free-to-play players cannot buy unlimited Stamina Potions or purchase Epic material bundles from the store. But with a disciplined routine, strategic event preparation, and smart material routing, F2P players can maintain fully Rare gear sets and steadily push into Epic territory within a realistic timeframe. This section lays out the complete F2P hunting playbook.

Weekly Hunting Routine

Structure your week around the event calendar rather than hunting arbitrarily. A typical week in Puzzles & Conquest follows a predictable event rotation:

Days 1–4 (Non-Event Days): Daily Stamina Farming

  • Target Monster: Hunt the highest tier you can kill cleanly in 1 march. For mid-game F2P players, this is T3 or low T4 Dragons (or whichever monster type matches your current Blacksmith priority).
  • Stamina Use: Spend all natural stamina regeneration. Do NOT use Stamina Potions. Bank every potion for event days.
  • Session Length: 10–15 minutes per session, twice daily (morning + evening). Enough to dump natural regen without idle overflow.
  • Material Routing: Check your Blacksmith queue each Monday. Set your weekly target monster based on what you need. Do not switch targets mid-week unless you complete the crafting goal early.

Days 5–7 (Pre-Event / Event Days): Event Push

  • Day 5 (Pre-Event Prep): Stop spending stamina 12–18 hours before the event goes live. Let your cap fill to maximum. Organize your Stamina Potion inventory — sort by size for deployment efficiency.
  • Day 6 (Event Day 1): Hunt the highest tier monster you can kill for maximum event points per stamina. T4 if capable, T3 if not. Deploy 50% of your saved Stamina Potions. Monitor the milestone progress — reach at least Milestone 2 before the halfway mark.
  • Day 7 (Event Day 2 / Post-Event): Deploy remaining Stamina Potions in the final 4 hours of the event for maximum leaderboard impact if top-30 is in reach. After event ends, return to normal daily farming routine with natural regen.

Monthly Material Farming Rotation

Rotate your primary target monster on a monthly basis based on your gear progression stage:

  1. Month 1–2 (New Player): Hunt T2/T3 Dragons exclusively. Goal: complete Common then Uncommon War Set. By end of month 2, target full Rare War Set weapon + armor.
  2. Month 3–4 (Mid-Game): Split hunting 70% Dragons (Rare War Set completion) / 30% Golems (Builder Set initiation). Goal: complete Rare War Set all 6 slots. Begin Rare Builder Set weapon.
  3. Month 5–6 (Transitioning to T4): Your march should now support T4 hunts. Shift primarily to T4 Dragons for Epic War Set material accumulation. Begin pushing Hunt ATK research to enable zero-casualty T4 kills.
  4. Month 7–9 (Epic Phase): Consistent T4 hunting generates Epic fragments. First Epic piece: Weapon. Then Armor. Builder/Tech Set Rare completion during this phase using Golem hunting in off-event periods.
  5. Month 10+ (Late-Game): T5 capability unlocked. Epic War Set nearly complete. Begin Serpent/Wraith rotation for accessory and specialty gear materials.

With consistent daily hunting and event participation, a disciplined F2P player can expect:

Common F2P Mistakes to Avoid

  • Hunting randomly without a target material in mind — Always know what you are hunting before spending stamina. Random hunting generates random materials that may not match your current crafting needs.
  • Spending Stamina Potions outside of events — Every potion spent on a non-event day is 2–5x less efficient than the same potion spent during a hunt event. Potions are strategic resources, not daily convenience items.
  • Hunting T1/T2 after Castle 10 — The opportunity cost of T1/T2 stamina is enormous. The same stamina on T3 generates 3–5x more valuable materials.
  • Ignoring Alliance Dragon participation — Missing Alliance Dragon attacks is leaving free credits and exclusive materials behind. Even logging in just to participate in dragon kills takes 2 minutes and provides disproportionate long-term value.
  • Not checking drop rates during events — Some events include temporary drop rate boosts for specific monster types. Missing these bonuses by hunting the wrong type during the boost window is a significant loss.
The F2P Compounding Advantage: The math behind F2P hunting is simple but powerful. A player who completes 60 T3 kills per day from natural regen generates approximately 1,800 kills per month. At a 1-in-10 Rare drop rate per kill, that is 180 Rare materials per month from zero spending. With drop rate research and event bonuses, that number grows to 250–300+ per month. Over 6 months, that is enough to fully craft and upgrade a complete Rare War Set and initiate Epic progression — purely from discipline and zero dollars spent.

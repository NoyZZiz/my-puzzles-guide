GEAR & EQUIPMENT GUIDE

Craft the right gear sets to dominate in combat, construction, and resource gathering

Gear Basics: How It Works

Gear is one of the most impactful power multipliers in Puzzles & Conquest, yet many players neglect it well into mid-game. Every hero can equip gear across 6 dedicated slots, each providing meaningful stat bonuses that stack to dramatically improve your hero's performance in any role — combat, construction, or research.

The 6 Gear Slots

  • Helmet — Primary defense and health bonuses; key for infantry survivability.
  • Armor — Troop defense and damage reduction; essential for frontline protection.
  • Weapon — The single highest-impact slot for Attack across all troop types. Always prioritize first.
  • Leggings — Secondary stat piece; contributes march speed and troop load for some sets.
  • Boots — March speed and troop speed; critical for Cavalry sets.
  • Accessory — Versatile bonus slot; contributes range and damage output for Ranged sets.

Crafting at the Blacksmith

All gear is crafted inside the Blacksmith building using materials collected from across the game. You do not need to level the Blacksmith extensively early on — even a low-level Blacksmith can produce Common and Uncommon gear. Higher Blacksmith levels unlock the ability to craft Rare and Epic tier pieces.

Where to Get Materials

  • Gathering resource tiles — Wood and Stone deposits frequently drop Blacksmith scraps as a secondary reward when your marches return.
  • Events — The weekly Gear/Relic/Runes rotation event (every Saturday) is your single best source of Rare and Epic-quality materials.
  • Monster kills — Different monster types drop different material sets. Dragon-type monsters specifically drop War Set materials.
  • Alliance Store — Material bundles can be purchased with Alliance Credits when stock is available. Check the store daily.

Gear Rarity Tiers

  • Common (White) — Easiest to craft, lowest stats. Only useful as a placeholder early on.
  • Uncommon (Green) — Step up from Common. Reasonable stats for early-game heroes.
  • Rare (Blue) — The mid-game target. Provides meaningful combat and utility bonuses. Aim for this as your baseline.
  • Epic (Purple) — Endgame priority. Dramatically superior stats. Materials are scarce — allocate them carefully.
Golden Rule: Always aim for at least Rare tier gear on your primary combat heroes. Epic gear is the endgame target, but Rare gear is fully viable for mid-game Military Expeditions and BOS participation. Never sit on materials — craft Rare now and upgrade to Epic as materials accumulate.
Warning: Do not invest enhancement stones into Common or Uncommon gear. You will need to re-craft at higher rarity and those enhancement materials cannot be recovered. Save all Warsagil stones exclusively for Rare and Epic pieces.

The Three Gear Sets

Unlike talent sets which require VIP unlocks to switch, gear can be swapped freely at any time. This makes situational gear switching one of the highest-value habits you can build. There are three primary gear configurations every serious player should maintain — each tuned for a completely different operational role.

War Set (Combat)

The War Set is your most important configuration and should be fully crafted first. It maximizes three critical combat stats: Troop Attack, Troop Defense, and Troop Health. When worn by your lead combat hero, it directly increases the killing power and survivability of every soldier in that hero's march.

  • Required for Military Expedition — underpowered gear means your march burns through troops unnecessarily.
  • Required for Battle of Supremacy (BOS) — every point of Attack matters in Alliance vs. Alliance confrontations.
  • Required for all PvP encounters — never enter an offensive or defensive battle without equipping your War Set first.
Mission-Critical: Your main combat hero must be wearing the War Set before any major battle launches. Forgetting to gear-switch before Military Expedition is one of the most common (and costly) mistakes in mid-game play. Build a pre-march checklist.

Builder Set (Construction)

The Builder Set reduces building and research times when worn by your lead hero. The reduction percentages may appear modest individually, but combined with the Production Talent Set and the Fast Build special talent, the cumulative time savings across thousands of upgrade hours become enormous.

  • Always equip the Builder Set on your lead hero before starting any long construction queue.
  • Stack with the Production Talent Set for maximum compounding efficiency.
  • The Fast Build special talent (activated while wearing the Builder Set) delivers the largest single burst of construction time savings — use it strategically on your longest single build.
Efficiency Protocol: Equip Builder Set → activate Fast Build talent → start the long build → switch back to War Set or default hero. The gear only needs to be equipped at the moment of starting the construction, not for its full duration.

Tech Set (Research)

The Tech Set functions identically to the Builder Set in principle, but its bonuses are optimized for reducing research time and cost rather than construction. Wear this set whenever you are queuing new research nodes in the Academy.

  • Works best combined with the Production Talent Set just as Builder Set does.
  • The material investment is the same tier as Builder Set — you can treat both as a shared "Infrastructure" investment tier, completed after your War Set is solid.
  • If resources are tight, prioritize Builder Set over Tech Set — building upgrades unlock higher-tier research prerequisites faster.
Set Priority Order: War Set → Builder Set → Tech Set. The War Set must come first because combat power gates your progress in Military Expedition and kingdom events. Infrastructure sets come second as quality-of-life accelerators.

War Set Priority: What to Craft First

Not all six gear slots contribute equally to your combat power. Spending your limited Epic materials on the wrong slot first is a common mid-game mistake. The optimal crafting order differs slightly depending on your primary troop type, but one rule applies universally: always craft the Weapon first.

Universal Law: The Weapon slot provides the single largest Attack stat boost of any individual gear piece, across all troop types. If you have enough materials for only one Epic piece, make it the Weapon every time.

Infantry War Set: Priority Order

  • 1. Weapon — Largest Attack boost. No exceptions.
  • 2. Helmet — Provides the strongest Defense boost for Infantry after the Weapon.
  • 3. Armor — Significant Attack and Defense combination; the third-highest-value Infantry slot.
  • 4. Leggings → Boots → Accessory — Secondary slots. Craft in any order once the top three are complete.

Infantry excels in holding defensive lines and absorbing damage. The Helmet and Armor priority reflects this: maximizing Defense means your Infantry survives longer, dealing more total damage before casualties mount.

Cavalry War Set: Priority Order

  • 1. Boots — March speed and Cavalry-specific Attack buffs. Cavalry lives and dies by speed; this is uniquely high-priority for the troop type.
  • 2. Weapon — Still the highest raw Attack piece. Slot 2 only because Boots unlock Cavalry's unique speed-attack synergy.
  • 3. Helmet — Defense and Health for Cavalry sustainability in extended engagements.
  • 4. Armor → Leggings → Accessory — Complete these as materials allow.

Cavalry operates on a speed-first philosophy. Fast marches mean better positioning in BOS and Military Expedition. Cavalry Boots frequently contain percentage-based march speed bonuses that no other slot provides — this is why they take the top priority slot for this troop type.

Ranged War Set: Priority Order

  • 1. Weapon — Ranged Attack multiplier is the highest-scaling stat for this troop type. Always first.
  • 2. Accessory — Provides percentage-based range and Ranged Attack bonuses unique to this slot. Critical for maximizing Ranged damage output.
  • 3. Helmet — Defense and Health to keep Ranged units alive long enough to deal sustained damage.
  • 4. Armor → Boots → Leggings — Complete remaining slots as materials become available.

Ranged troops deal the highest burst damage per unit but have the lowest Defense. Maximizing their Attack output before investing in Defense is the correct priority — if your Ranged units hit hard enough, engagements end before Defense becomes the limiting factor.

Mixed Troop Commanders: If your march composition mixes troop types, default to the Infantry priority order. The Helmet → Armor → Weapon sequence provides the broadest stat coverage for mixed marches. Specialized Cavalry or Ranged orders are only optimal when running a dedicated single-type march.

Curios: The Hidden Power Layer

Most players understand the 6-slot gear system. Far fewer fully leverage Curios — the parallel accessory layer that operates independently from standard gear and provides percentage-based buffs that can dwarf the bonuses from normal equipment. Understanding Curios is what separates solid mid-game players from genuine endgame operators.

Curios are equipped in their own separate slot, completely independent of your 6 standard gear slots. This means a hero fully geared in Epic War Set pieces can additionally equip an Epic Curio on top — compounding buffs, not replacing them. Epic Curios represent the highest-priority long-term investment in the Blacksmith progression system.

How to Craft Curios

Navigate to Blacksmith → Heroic Curio tab. Curios are crafted using Runestones, which are primarily obtained from the Hell Fortress event. Runestone accumulation is time-gated, so starting Curio investment as early as possible is always the right call — even if you can only craft Common Curios initially.

Key Curios to Target

MYTHIC TIER

Energy Scepter Curio (Mythic) — The single most game-changing Curio in the game for Military Expedition players. When equipped on Horus at Level 131 with +8 enhancement, it grants +200,000 Infirmary capacity. This is not a marginal improvement — it fundamentally changes your ability to absorb casualties in Military Expedition without permanent troop loss. If you run ME regularly, this is your #1 Curio target bar none.

EPIC TIER — COMBAT
  • Dragon Scale Curio — Boosts your hero's troop Attack by a flat percentage. Craft this for your main combat hero. The Attack percentage scales with enhancement level, making a +6 or higher Dragon Scale Curio significantly more impactful than an un-enhanced version. This is the standard combat Curio recommendation for non-Horus heroes.
  • Warlord Curio — Increases your march size, allowing more troops per march. This directly increases your army's damage output per engagement and is especially valuable for BOS rallies where march cap determines battle outcome.
Curio Enhancement is Critical: Unlike standard gear where enhancement is a bonus optimization, Curio enhancement directly unlocks threshold effects. The Energy Scepter Curio's +200k Infirmary bonus is only active at +8 enhancement. Under-enhanced Mythic Curios are dramatically less effective than their potential. Always enhance your primary Curio to at least +6 before crafting secondary ones.
Runestone Management: Runestones are the bottleneck for Curio crafting. Participate in every Hell Fortress event to maximize accumulation. Do not spend Runestones on Common or Uncommon Curios — save them until you can craft Rare or Epic tier Curios directly. The efficiency loss from crafting low-tier Curios with premium materials is significant.

Crafting Materials: Where to Get Them

Material acquisition is the core constraint on your gear progression. Unlike resources (Food, Wood, Stone, Gold) which regenerate passively, Blacksmith materials must be actively farmed from specific sources. Knowing where each rarity tier of material comes from lets you plan your progression timeline realistically rather than assuming Epic gear arrives quickly.

Common & Uncommon Materials

These materials are primarily acquired through resource tile gathering. When your march returns from a Wood or Stone deposit, there is a chance for Blacksmith scrap materials to drop as secondary loot. The drop rate is low per gather, but with consistent marching — sending all available marches on gather rotations daily — the accumulation is steady. These materials are sufficient for Common and Uncommon gear crafting and should never be your bottleneck.

  • Send all idle marches to resource tiles daily, even when not actively playing.
  • Higher-level resource tiles (Lv4, Lv5) have better secondary drop rates.
  • Alliance territory bonus tiles often have improved drop rates — prioritize gathering within Alliance territory.

Event Materials (Primary Source for Rare & Epic)

The weekly Saturday Gear/Relic/Runes event is your primary source of Rare and Epic Blacksmith materials. This event rotates in a predictable cycle and rewards materials heavily for completing event objectives. Missing this event consistently is the single biggest reason players' gear progression stalls in mid-game.

  • Plan your weekend gaming time around this event — high participation directly translates to faster Epic gear timelines.
  • Complete all available event tiers, not just the first few. Later tiers disproportionately reward Rare and Epic materials.
  • Store event materials without crafting until you have enough for your next priority piece (e.g., Epic Weapon).
Event Calendar Tip: Check the Event Calendar section of this site to track when the Gear event is next in rotation. The 3-event cycle (Gear → Relic → Runes) repeats predictably — knowing when Gear event hits next lets you prepare by stockpiling Blacksmith tokens and event participation items in advance.

Monster Kills

Killing world monsters drops materials specific to each monster type. This creates a deliberate farm loop: Dragon-type monsters drop War Set materials specifically. If you are prioritizing your War Set (which you should be), target Dragon monsters with your daily stamina allocation.

  • Match your stamina target to your current gear priority: Dragon monsters for War Set, other types for Infrastructure sets.
  • Higher monster levels drop higher rarity materials. Kill the highest level you can reliably defeat.
  • Alliance Hunt events multiply material drops significantly — coordinate with your alliance to maximize kill rate during these windows.

Alliance Store

The Alliance Store periodically stocks Blacksmith material bundles purchasable with Alliance Credits. These bundles are efficient purchases when available — Alliance Credits are earned through donations and alliance events that you should already be doing for other reasons, making store materials effectively free secondary rewards.

  • Check the Alliance Store daily — material bundles rotate in and out of stock.
  • Prioritize purchasing Rare and Epic material bundles over Common bundles. Your Common material supply from gathering should already be sufficient.
Resource Allocation Rule: Save all purple (Epic) materials exclusively for your primary combat hero's War Set pieces, in Weapon-first order. Do not spread Epic materials across multiple heroes or use them on Infrastructure set pieces until your main hero's War Set is fully Epic-tier. Diluted investment means no hero reaches peak effectiveness.

Enhancing Gear

Crafting gear at its base rarity is only the beginning. Every piece of gear can be enhanced from +1 up to +12 using Warsagil stones. Each enhancement level increases the gear's stats by a fixed increment, meaning a +12 Epic Weapon dramatically outperforms a +0 Epic Weapon. Enhancement is the long-term optimization layer on top of your crafting progression.

Enhancement Efficiency Rules

  • Never enhance Common or Uncommon gear. Warsagil stones are a limited resource and the stats gained from enhancing low-rarity gear are too small to justify. You will replace Common/Uncommon gear long before reaching high enhancement levels — those stones are permanently spent.
  • Rare gear enhancement is acceptable but suboptimal. If you are stuck at Rare tier for an extended period, enhancing your Rare Weapon to +4 or +6 is reasonable to bridge the gap. Stop when you have enough Epic materials ready and transition immediately.
  • Epic gear is the primary enhancement target. Enhancement is most cost-efficient applied to Epic-tier gear because the base stats are highest, meaning each enhancement increment adds more absolute stat value than the same increment on lower-rarity gear.

Curio Enhancement: A Special Case

Curio enhancement follows the same stone-based system but has critically different stakes. Some Curios have threshold effects — bonuses that only activate at specific enhancement levels. The most important example in the current meta:

  • Energy Scepter Curio: The full +200,000 Infirmary capacity bonus only activates at +8 enhancement. Below this level, the Curio provides a smaller Infirmary bonus, making the achievement of exactly +8 a specific milestone worth investing toward aggressively.

Priority Enhancement Order

  1. Epic Weapon → +6 — Your combat hero's Weapon to +6 before touching any other slot. This maximizes the highest-value stat gain per stone spent.
  2. Primary Curio → +8 — If you have an Epic Curio (especially Energy Scepter), push it to its threshold enhancement level.
  3. Epic Helmet → +4 — Second gear slot to receive enhancement investment.
  4. Epic Armor → +4 — Third slot.
  5. Remaining Epic slots → +2 to +4 — Fill remaining slots with baseline enhancement.
  6. Weapon → +12 — Return to Weapon and push it to max once all other slots have baseline enhancement.
Stone Conservation Tip: Warsagil stones are most frequently obtained from events and the Alliance Store. Track your stone inventory relative to your next enhancement milestone and avoid impulse-spending on low-priority pieces. Discipline in stone allocation is what separates players who reach +12 Epic Weapons from those perpetually stuck at +2.
Enhancement does not reset on gear replacement. If you craft a new Epic piece to replace an existing one, the new piece starts at +0. Plan your enhancement investment to align with your crafting roadmap — enhance a piece only once you are confident it will not be immediately replaced.

Relics: Alliance-Wide Buffs

Relics operate on a different tier entirely from individual hero gear. Crafted inside the Alliance Blacksmith (separate from your personal Blacksmith), Relics provide powerful buffs that apply to all alliance members simultaneously. They represent the collective power of your entire alliance directed toward shared strategic objectives.

What Relics Provide

The most impactful Relics in rotation typically boost:

  • Training Speed — Accelerates troop training for all members; critical during active kingdom development phases.
  • March Size — Increases the maximum troop count per march for all members; directly amplifies combat power in BOS rallies and Military Expedition.
  • Troop Attack — Flat percentage Attack bonus applied on top of all individual gear and talent buffs; scales multiplicatively with your personal power investment.

How Relics Are Crafted

Relic crafting requires Alliance Resources — a special currency donated by members into the Alliance treasury. The R5 (Leader) and R4 (Officer) ranks control which Relic is selected and initiated. Individual members contribute by donating Alliance Resources consistently through the donation mechanic.

Member Responsibility: Donate Alliance Resources every day, without exception. Relics provide free power to every member — including you. Every donation you skip is shared power you are leaving on the table. Even small daily donations compound into significant Relic uptime over a month of consistent play.

Relic Selection Strategy

If you have R4 or R5 authority and control Relic selection, prioritize based on your alliance's current objective:

  • During BOS season: prioritize March Size and Troop Attack Relics — combat effectiveness is the decisive factor.
  • During development phases between events: prioritize Training Speed Relics — rebuilding troop numbers faster accelerates recovery from combat losses.
  • During Military Expedition: March Size Relics compound with individual hero gear and Warlord Curios to maximize march capacity.
Relic Stacking: Relic buffs stack with your personal gear, Curio, and talent buffs. A March Size Relic + Warlord Curio + Mega March talent active simultaneously can push your march count to dramatically higher numbers than any single source alone. Identify your alliance's key events and ensure the right Relic is active during them.

Gear Switching Strategy

Like talent sets, gear should be treated as a situational tool rather than a static loadout. Wearing the same gear configuration 24/7 means you are under-powered in combat half the time and over-powered for building (a wasted opportunity) the other half. Situational gear switching is the habit that distinguishes strategic players from autopilot players.

The Core Switching Protocol

  • Before construction or research: Equip the Builder Set or Tech Set on your lead hero, activate Fast Build talent if applicable, queue the construction, then switch back. The gear only needs to be active when the job starts — not for its entire duration.
  • Before any battle: Equip the full War Set on all five combat heroes. Check every hero slot — a single hero running Builder Set gear in a Military Expedition march represents significant lost combat power.
  • After battle: No mandatory switch required. Unlike some mechanics, gear does not wear out or degrade through use. There is no penalty for keeping War Set equipped indefinitely between battles.

Mid-March Gear Switching: What You Need to Know

You can switch gear on a hero who is currently in an active march — the game permits this. However, there is a critical timing rule: new gear stats only apply to the next march, not the current one. Stats are locked in at the moment the march launches, not updated dynamically mid-flight.

  • Switching gear while a march is en route does nothing for that march's combat performance.
  • However, you can use this window productively — switch to Builder Set, start a queued construction, then your next march will launch fresh with the correct gear equipped if you switch back before initiating it.
Pre-Launch Checklist for BOS and Military Expedition: Always gear-check your heroes BEFORE launching your first march. Once that march is away, the stats are locked. A pre-march gear audit takes 30 seconds and can be the difference between a successful ME clear and a costly troop loss. Make it a non-negotiable part of your battle launch routine.
Five-Hero Audit: BOS and Military Expedition use all 5 hero slots simultaneously. Audit all 5 heroes' gear before launch — not just your lead hero. It's common to forget to switch secondary heroes off Builder/Tech Set after a construction session. A fully geared march performs dramatically better than one with two heroes accidentally wearing Infrastructure gear.

Gear Switching vs. Talent Set Switching

Gear switching and talent set switching work independently and stack together. The optimal routine before a major battle is: switch all 5 heroes to War Set gear and switch your talent set to Attack Set. Doing both doubles the benefit relative to doing only one. Build both habits in parallel rather than sequentially — the learning curve for both is low once you internalize the pre-march checklist.

F2P Gear Strategy

As a free-to-play player, you will not have Epic gear on day one, day thirty, or likely even day ninety. That is normal — and it is not a competitive disadvantage if you play the long game correctly. The F2P gear path is slower than a spender's, but it is not fundamentally different. The same priority rules apply; the timeline is simply extended. What destroys F2P gear progression is impatience: rushing purchases, making wrong investments, or spreading materials too thin.

The Four-Step F2P Gear Roadmap

1
CRAFT UNCOMMON WAR SET FOR ALL 5 COMBAT HEROES

Uncommon materials are abundant from gathering. Getting all 5 heroes into a full Uncommon War Set is fast, cheap, and provides a significant baseline combat power jump over no gear at all. Do this first — it costs almost nothing relative to the power gained.

2
UPGRADE MAIN HERO TO RARE WAR SET (WEAPON FIRST)

Focus all Rare materials on a single hero — your most powerful combat hero. Craft the Weapon first, then proceed through the priority order for your troop type. Do not split Rare materials across multiple heroes. One hero at Rare outperforms five heroes at the Uncommon/Rare mixed boundary.

3
ACCUMULATE EPIC MATERIALS THROUGH WEEKEND GEAR EVENTS

Do not craft Epic pieces the moment you have enough materials for one. Save until you have enough for at least the Weapon plus one secondary piece (e.g., Helmet). This prevents the trap of spending all Epic materials on one minor slot while the Weapon waits months. Stockpile with a goal in mind.

4
CRAFT EPIC WEAPON → EPIC HELMET → EPIC ARMOR IN ORDER

When you have sufficient Epic materials, follow the priority order strictly. Epic Weapon first, Helmet second, Armor third. Complete these three before spending a single Epic material on the remaining three slots. The top three slots provide the largest percentage of total War Set power — completing them gives you roughly 75% of full Epic War Set performance.

The Diamond Trap: Never rush to Epic by spending diamonds on material packs from the shop. The price-to-power ratio is extremely poor for gear materials in the premium shop. The difference between Rare and Epic gear is earned through consistent weekend event participation over 3 to 6 months of play — this is by design. Players who accept this timeline and invest event participation earn it; players who panic-spend diamonds for marginal acceleration burn resources they need elsewhere.
Compete on Consistency, Not Spending: F2P players who log in daily, participate in every Gear event, hunt Dragon monsters with daily stamina, and donate Alliance Resources every day will have better gear at the 6-month mark than casual spenders who buy material packs sporadically. Consistent participation beats intermittent investment. This is one of the most F2P-friendly systems in the game if you engage with it systematically.

Long-Term F2P Milestones

  • Month 1–2: All 5 heroes in Uncommon War Set. Main hero hitting Rare Weapon.
  • Month 3–4: Main hero in full Rare War Set. Epic materials accumulating via weekend events.
  • Month 5–6: Main hero crafts Epic Weapon. Begin Rare Builder/Tech Set for infrastructure heroes.
  • Month 6+: Main hero progresses through Epic Helmet and Armor. Begin Curio investment with accumulated Runestones.
Patience Pays Compound Interest: Every Gear event participated in, every Dragon monster hunted, and every Alliance Resource donated contributes to a material stockpile that accelerates exponentially. A player at month 6 who has engaged consistently will find Epic pieces arriving faster than at month 3 — the investment compounds. Stay the course.

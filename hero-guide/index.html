HERO GUIDE & TIER LIST

Master hero selection, squad formation, and upgrade priorities for maximum combat power

Understanding Heroes

Written by: Noyzzing

Heroes are the backbone of your military power in Puzzles & Conquest. Every march you send into battle is amplified or diminished by the heroes you choose, the level they are at, and how well you have enhanced them. Understanding the fundamentals before you spend a single resource is critical — hero investment is measured in months, not days.

Star Ratings: What Actually Matters

Heroes come in ratings from 1-star to 5-star. In practical terms, only 4-star and 5-star heroes have any relevance for endgame play. 1, 2, and 3-star heroes provide negligible buffs and should only be used as placeholders in the early game before you have unlocked your core roster. Do not invest soul stones, fragments, or significant XP potions into anything below 4-star.

Troop Type Alignment

Each hero is inherently linked to one of three troop types: Infantry, Cavalry, or Ranged. A hero aligned to Infantry will buff Infantry troops specifically — their passive skills, active abilities, and talent bonuses are oriented around Infantry combat. Placing an Infantry hero in a Cavalry march provides almost no military benefit. This alignment is the single most important concept to understand before building your squad.

There is a special category called Combo Heroes (such as Aleria), whose buffs apply universally regardless of troop type. These are the exception and are exceptionally valuable precisely because they transcend the troop-type restriction.

Key Evolution Breakpoints

Hero power does not scale linearly — it spikes dramatically at specific evolution thresholds. These are the level gates that matter most:

  • Level 31 (2-Star Evolution): First evolution gate. Minor stat increase. Primarily a stepping stone.
  • Level 71 (3-Star Evolution): Unlocks additional passive skills. Notable bump in effectiveness.
  • Level 131 (4-Star & 5-Star Evolution): This is the endgame threshold. At Level 131, a fully evolved 4-star or 5-star hero unlocks massive buffs that dwarf everything before it. Combined with Mythic gear, Level 131 is the only goal worth planning around.
Level 131 + Mythic Gear: When a hero reaches Level 131 and is equipped with Mythic-tier gear in the appropriate slot, they unlock a special synergy buff tier. For example, Horus at Level 131 with a Mythic Energy Scepter Curio and +8 enhancement can grant over +200,000 infirmary capacity — a stat of enormous importance during Military Expedition.

Two Separate Upgrade Systems

Heroes have two distinct progression mechanics that many players confuse or conflate:

  • XP Potions (Leveling): Using XP potions raises your hero's base stats — attack, defense, HP — proportionally to their level. This is the primary driver of raw combat numbers. XP potions are a scarce resource; do not waste them on heroes below your top 2 or 3 priority targets.
  • Fragment Enhancement: Enhancing with hero fragments unlocks and upgrades Troop Skills. These are passive bonuses that are permanently active in battle regardless of whether the hero is your leading march hero or a support slot. Fragment enhancement is particularly important for economy heroes like Horus and Ryska, whose passive bonuses (infirmary capacity, training cost reduction) operate at all times.
Key Insight: Troop Skills unlocked via fragment enhancement are always-on passives. This means you can enhance Horus's fragments and receive his infirmary buff permanently without ever deploying him in a combat march. Enhancement and leveling are parallel tracks — max both for your priority heroes.

The 5-Hero Squad Rule

Every march you send into battle has five hero slots. How you fill those five slots is the single most impactful tactical decision you make on a per-march basis. Most new players fill slots randomly or simply slot in their five highest-level heroes — this is a significant mistake that dramatically reduces combat effectiveness.

The Correct Formation

The correct squad composition follows one simple rule: 4 heroes matching your main troop type + 1 Combo Hero.

If you march with Cavalry troops, slots 1 through 4 should be Cavalry heroes (Atlas, Morgana, Odin, and a 4th cavalry option). Slot 5 is reserved for Aleria or another Combo Hero. This formation ensures maximum buff stacking on the troop type you actually brought to battle.

Why Not 5 of the Same Type?

Never fill all five slots with heroes of the same troop type. Hero buff stacking has diminishing returns, and certain hero passive buffs do not stack when multiple heroes of the identical subtype are present in the same march. The 5th slot's buffs from a second copy of a hero type are wasted. The Combo Hero fills that slot with a unique, non-overlapping set of buffs that always adds value on top of your 4 main-type heroes.

Never Mix Troop-Type Heroes Randomly

Bringing an Infantry hero on a Cavalry march provides essentially zero combat benefit. Infantry hero passives buff Infantry-specific stats (infantry attack multiplier, infantry defense rating, etc.) — none of which apply to Cavalry troops. A random Infantry hero in a Cavalry march is literally a wasted slot: you have 4 effective heroes instead of 5.

Critical Rule: Your march troop type and your hero types must match. Before hitting march, always verify: (1) What troop type am I bringing? (2) Are 4 of my 5 heroes aligned to that type? (3) Is my 5th slot a Combo Hero? If any answer is no, fix it before marching.

The Combo Hero: Always Slot 5

The Combo Hero's buffs are explicitly designed to apply regardless of troop type. Aleria's bonuses — whether attack, HP, or specialty effects — function identically whether your march is Infantry, Cavalry, or Ranged. This universal applicability makes her (and other Combo Heroes like Phoenix) the mandatory occupants of that final slot in every serious combat march you send.

Pro Tip: Set up saved squad presets for each march type. Create an "INF Squad," a "CAV Squad," and a "RNG Squad" and save them in-game. Before dispatching, simply select the preset that matches your troop composition. This eliminates the risk of accidentally sending the wrong heroes at the most critical moment.

Hero Upgrade Priority

Hero leveling is one of the most resource-intensive long-term investments in Puzzles & Conquest. XP potions, soul stones, and enhancement fragments are all scarce commodities. A clear priority order prevents you from spreading resources thin across a dozen heroes and ultimately maxing out none of them.

The Priority Ladder

1

Main Combat Hero to Level 131

This is a months-long project and your absolute first priority. Choose your main troop type (the troop type your alliance rallies with, or the type you have the most of), and identify the S or A-tier hero for that type (e.g., Atlas for Cavalry, Medusa for Infantry, Artemis for Ranged). Every XP potion, every soul stone, goes here first. Do not diversify until this is done.

2

Aleria (Combo Hero) to Playable Level

Your Combo Hero must be leveled enough to contribute meaningfully in your squad. She does not need to reach Lv131 before your main combat hero, but she should not be sitting at Lv1 while your combat heroes are pushing Lv100. Invest secondary resources into Aleria as a concurrent priority.

3

Economy Heroes: Horus & Ryska Fragments

Horus and Ryska's most critical value comes from their Troop Skills, unlocked via fragment enhancement — not their level. Prioritize enhancing their fragments to unlock the passive infirmary capacity (Horus) and training cost reduction (Ryska) bonuses. These passives work around the clock without deploying them in combat.

4

Economy Heroes: Horus to Level 131

Once your main combat hero is at Lv131, begin pushing Horus toward Lv131. The Lv131 milestone for Horus — combined with Mythic gear — is the gateway to the +200k infirmary capacity that makes surviving Military Expedition manageable. This is the second major long-term leveling project.

5

Remaining Squad Heroes

After the above, fill out your full 4-hero main-type squad by leveling the remaining 3 combat heroes at your main troop type. Use leftover XP potions after weekly events. Do not rush these — their contribution is meaningful but secondary to your top 2-3 heroes.

Soul Stones and Evolution

Soul Stones are used to evolve heroes through their star rating gates. You will need a significant accumulation of soul stones to push a hero from 3-star to 4-star and from 4-star to 5-star. Avoid spending soul stones on experimental heroes — save them for your designated Lv131 targets. Spending soul stones on a hero you will ultimately abandon is one of the most costly mistakes in the game.

Save Your XP Potions: Only pour XP potions into your top 2-3 heroes. Using them on a B-tier or economy hero is a waste of a critical resource. Economy heroes like Ryska gain their value from fragment enhancement, not level — do not XP-level them past the minimum needed to equip their gear slots.

Economy Heroes You Must Build

Economy heroes are the unsung MVPs of Puzzles & Conquest progression. They do not kill enemy troops directly. They do not win single battles on their own. But over the course of weeks and months of play, their passive bonuses compound into advantages that determine whether you can sustain an endgame-level army. Ignoring them is one of the most common and costly mistakes in this game.

HORUS
RANGED HERO • INFIRMARY SPECIALIST • S-TIER ECONOMY

Horus is arguably the most important long-term hero investment for any player who participates in Military Expedition. His passive Troop Skills, unlocked through fragment enhancement, directly increase your infirmary bed count — the resource that determines how many wounded troops you can save after a battle rather than losing them permanently.

The full Horus power spike requires a specific combination: Level 131 + Mythic Energy Scepter Curio equipped + +8 fragment enhancement. With all three conditions met, Horus provides an additional +200,000 infirmary capacity. In the context of Military Expedition, where you may take 100,000+ wounds in a single defensive engagement, this extra capacity is the difference between losing 50,000 troops permanently or saving all of them.

RYSKA
ECONOMY HERO • TRAINING SPECIALIST • A-TIER (S-TIER FOR F2P)

Ryska directly reduces the resource cost of troop training and accelerates training queue speed. At maximum enhancement, Ryska reduces training costs by up to 25%. Over the course of a month of consistent troop production, this translates to an enormous resource saving — equivalent to weeks of resource gathering for free.

In combination with training speed events (especially when the Fast Comeback buff is active post-ME, granting 300% training speed), Ryska allows you to rebuild your army dramatically faster than commanders who neglected her. Fast army rebuilding is the defining competitive advantage in alliance-level warfare — the player who replaces losses fastest dictates the tempo of the next conflict.

F2P Strategy: If you are free-to-play or low spender, build Horus and Ryska alongside your main combat hero. Their always-on passive benefits compound every single day. A well-built Horus prevents catastrophic troop losses in ME; a well-built Ryska rebuilds your army faster than your opponents. These two heroes multiply the value of everything else you do.

Hero Gear (Equipment)

Each hero in Puzzles & Conquest has six equipment slots that can be filled with crafted gear. Hero gear is one of the most impactful stat multipliers in the game — a fully geared hero at Level 100 will outperform an ungeared hero at Level 131 in many metrics. Gear is crafted in the Blacksmith using materials sourced from events, gathering, and alliance shops.

The War Set: Top Combat Gear

The War Set is the highest-tier combat gear set in the game. Crafting a full War Set for your main combat hero is the gear priority above all others. Each piece of the War Set provides superior attack, defense, and HP bonuses compared to lower-tier sets, and completing the full set unlocks a set bonus that further amplifies combat effectiveness.

War Set pieces require rare materials that are primarily obtained from major events, gathering expeditions, and the alliance tech shop. Do not attempt to craft War Set gear casually — plan material accumulation around event cycles that reward the specific materials you need.

Gear Priority Order

  • Priority 1 — Main Combat Hero's War Set: Craft the full War Set for your primary Lv131 combat hero first. This is non-negotiable for any commander who rallies or participates in BOS or ME at a serious level.
  • Priority 2 — Horus's Mythic Energy Scepter Curio: The Curio slot for Horus is critical for unlocking the +200k infirmary capacity. This specific Mythic Curio is as important as any combat gear piece. Prioritize its materials alongside your War Set farm.
  • Priority 3 — Combo Hero Gear: Equip Aleria with whatever War Set pieces you can afford after your main combat hero is fully geared.
  • Priority 4 — Remaining Squad Heroes: Fill in gear for your remaining 3 combat squad heroes using lower-tier sets until you accumulate materials for their War Sets.
Rally Leader Rule: Always verify that your Rally Leader hero is equipped with their full War Set before initiating a major rally or ME Altar attack. Launching an alliance rally without your rally leader fully geared is one of the most costly mistakes a commander can make — it reduces the entire rally's combat power significantly.
Blacksmith Tip: The Blacksmith crafts gear, but material farming is the bottleneck. Always track which events reward the specific Blacksmith materials you need for your current crafting target. Running events specifically for those materials is far more efficient than waiting for random drops. Set a weekly material-farming goal and stick to it.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

The hero system has enough depth that players at every experience level make recurring mistakes. The following are the most costly, most common errors — understanding them will save you weeks of misdirected resource investment and prevent avoidable losses in critical events.

Mixing troop types in one march. Sending a Cavalry march with Infantry and Ranged heroes filling the hero slots. Your heroes must match your troop type. An Infantry hero buffing a Cavalry march provides near-zero combat value — it's a wasted slot every time.

Forgetting to switch to the Attack talent set before battle. Many players keep their talent trees on Development/Balance mode for resource production and forget to switch to the War talent tree before marching into combat. This is a massive attack and defense reduction at the most critical moment. Set a pre-battle checklist.

Using diamonds to level heroes instead of saving for Infirmary upgrades. Diamond spending on hero XP is one of the worst ROI uses in the game. Diamonds should be reserved for Infirmary expansions, VIP maintenance, and critical acceleration of timed events. Hero leveling is done with XP potions earned through gameplay and events.

Ignoring economy heroes entirely. Spending all resources on combat heroes and never building Horus or Ryska. Horus's infirmary buff and Ryska's training cost reduction are permanent, always-active bonuses that compound enormous value over time. A player who builds both will sustain an army that a pure combat-hero investor simply cannot match long-term.

Sending marches without a Combo Hero. Filling all 5 slots with troop-type heroes means you lose the universal buff contribution that Aleria or Phoenix provides. The 5th slot is free real estate for an additional layer of buffs — leaving it filled with a 5th same-type hero wastes that opportunity due to buff overlap and diminishing returns.

Spreading XP potions across too many heroes. Having 10 heroes at Level 60 is drastically worse than having 2 heroes at Level 120. The Level 131 breakpoint is an exponential buff spike — it requires concentrated resource investment on a few heroes to reach. Spreading thin means no hero ever reaches endgame potential.

Launching alliance rallies without checking the rally leader's gear. The rally leader hero's stats are the foundation of the entire rally's combat calculation. Marching an alliance rally with an ungeared or low-level rally leader hero undermines the contributions of every alliance member in that rally. Always inspect gear before leading.

Quick Pre-Battle Checklist: Before every significant march — (1) Troop type selected matches hero squad type? (2) Talent tree switched to War/Attack set? (3) 5th slot has Combo Hero? (4) Rally leader has full War Set equipped? (5) Infirmary capacity checked and T1 fillers in place for ME? If all five check out, you are ready to march.

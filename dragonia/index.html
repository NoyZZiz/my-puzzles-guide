DRAGONIA SYSTEM

DRAGON SYSTEM GUIDE

How to unlock, level, and use your Dragon to dominate the battlefield

What is the Dragon System?

The Dragon System — also known as Dragonia — is one of the most impactful progression systems in Puzzles & Conquest. Unlike buildings or research that simply provide passive stat boosts, your Dragon is a living companion that grows alongside your castle, provides powerful persistent buffs to your troops, and can be deployed as an active force multiplier in battle. Mastering the Dragon System is one of the clearest dividing lines between average commanders and truly formidable ones.

At its core, the Dragon System operates around three pillars: Dragon Level (how powerful your dragon is overall), Dragon Skills (the specific buffs your dragon provides), and Dragon Shards (the primary currency used to level skills and gain experience). Everything in Dragonia flows through these three pillars, and understanding how they interact is essential to building an efficient Dragon progression plan.

Dragons as Troop Companions

Your Dragon is not just a cosmetic feature — it is a fully integrated combat system. Once unlocked, your Dragon provides passive stat bonuses to your troops at all times. These bonuses are applied directly to the troop stats of whichever march type your dragon is assigned to, stacking multiplicatively with research, gear, and hero skill bonuses. A fully-leveled Dragon with maxed Attack skills can provide:

  • 30–60% Attack bonus to your primary troop type, depending on skill investment
  • 20–40% Defense bonus applied passively to marches you lead
  • 15–30% HP bonus that increases your troops' survivability in every engagement
  • Active battle skills that trigger during combat for additional burst damage or mitigation

Dragon Levels: The 1–30 Structure

Your Dragon progresses from Level 1 to Level 30, with each level requiring increasing amounts of Dragon EXP obtained from Dragon Shards. The level range is divided into five meaningful phases:

  • Levels 1–5 (Hatchling Phase): Your Dragon is weak but every level-up unlocks new skill slots. Focus entirely on unlocking your first Attack skill during this phase.
  • Levels 6–10 (Juvenile Phase): Skill bonuses start to become noticeable. This is your first major power spike target as a new player.
  • Levels 11–15 (Young Dragon Phase): Multiple skill trees become available. The gap between focused players and unfocused players begins to open here.
  • Levels 16–20 (Adult Phase): Dragon buffs are now substantial enough to swing the outcome of player-vs-player battles. A Dragon at level 20 with a focused skill path provides buffs comparable to a full tier of Military research nodes.
  • Levels 21–30 (Elder Phase): The elite tier of Dragon progression. Players with Elder-phase Dragons hold a significant advantage in all forms of combat and are nearly unmatchable by players with neglected Dragon Systems.

Dragon Shards: The Core Currency

Dragon Shards are the lifeblood of the Dragon System. Every Dragon action — leveling up, unlocking skills, upgrading skills — costs Dragon Shards. They are deliberately limited in supply, which means every shard you spend is an investment decision. The difference between a smart shard spender and an uninformed one can amount to weeks or even months of effective progress.

Dragon Shards are not universally interchangeable. Some skill nodes require specific Elemental Shards (Fire, Ice, Storm) while the base level and basic skills use Standard Dragon Shards. Understanding which shard types to stock up on before committing to a skill path will save you significant frustration.

System Note: The Dragon System is entirely separate from your Hero system and your Research system. Dragon buffs do not conflict with Hero skills — they stack additively with Hero Attack buffs and multiplicatively with Research bonuses, making all three systems synergistic rather than competitive.

Unlocking Your Dragon

Unlocking your Dragon is a milestone event that changes your game permanently. Unlike many systems that open gradually, the Dragon System becomes available in a single gated moment — when your Castle reaches the required headquarters level. From that point forward, your Dragon is always with you, always buffing, always growing.

Castle Level Requirement

The Dragon System unlocks when your Headquarters reaches Level 15. This is a deliberate design choice: by the time you've built to HQ 15, you should have a basic understanding of your troop preference and resource management. Players who rush to HQ 15 purely to unlock the Dragon will find the system accessible, but players who arrive at HQ 15 with well-developed resource production will be able to invest in the Dragon System much more aggressively from day one.

Before pushing your HQ to 15, make sure these supporting buildings are also upgraded to match:

  • Dragon Den: Must be built and upgraded in tandem with your HQ. This is the building that houses your Dragon and governs its maximum level cap.
  • Barracks (minimum level 12): You will need troop production running at full speed to participate in Dragon Raids as soon as you unlock the system.
  • Academy (minimum level 14): Dragon-related research nodes in both Development and Military trees require a high-level Academy.

Claiming Your First Dragon

After reaching HQ 15, a quest chain triggers that walks you through the Dragon unlock sequence:

  1. Build the Dragon Den: The Dragon Den appears in your build menu as a new unlocked structure. Place it in your castle grounds — it takes approximately 24–48 hours to complete construction at standard speed. Use speedups if you have them.
  2. Collect the Dragon Egg: A quest reward delivers your first Dragon Egg. Open it from your Items inventory to receive your Dragon at Level 1.
  3. Name Your Dragon: This is cosmetic but satisfying. Your dragon's name displays during Dragon Raids and on some leaderboards.
  4. Complete the Tutorial Raid: A guided Dragon Raid tutorial introduces the mechanics with no risk — completing it rewards your first batch of Dragon Shards (typically 50–100 Standard Shards).

The Dragon Den Building

The Dragon Den is more than just the building that unlocks your Dragon — it is an ongoing upgrade investment that directly caps your Dragon's maximum level. The relationship is strict:

  • Dragon Den Level 1–3: Dragon max level capped at 10
  • Dragon Den Level 4–6: Dragon max level capped at 20
  • Dragon Den Level 7–9: Dragon max level capped at 25
  • Dragon Den Level 10: Dragon max level uncapped — Dragon can reach Level 30
Critical Trap to Avoid: Do not let Dragon Shards pile up without spending them just because you are still leveling your Dragon Den. While it is true that the Den level gates your Dragon's level cap, spending shards on skill upgrades does NOT require your Dragon to be at max level. You can and should invest shards into skill nodes at any Dragon level — skills level independently from the Dragon's overall level.

Dragon Den Upgrade Path

The Dragon Den upgrades follow the same resource model as other major buildings: Food, Wood, Stone, and Iron, with upgrade timers that increase at each tier. Key milestones to prioritize in your building queue:

  • Dragon Den Level 3 — Unlock as quickly as possible after building the Den. The increased Dragon EXP cap means your early Shard investments convert to meaningful levels rather than sitting at the ceiling.
  • Dragon Den Level 6 — Target before your Dragon reaches Level 18 so you are never blocked from leveling. Den upgrades can take 3–7 days each at this tier; plan ahead.
  • Dragon Den Level 10 — The endgame target. Reserve major speedup stashes for Den upgrades Level 8–10, as these have the longest timers and unlock the most powerful Dragon levels.
Commander's Tip: Always have a Dragon Den upgrade queued in your building pipeline. Unlike research, which competes with other development priorities, the Dragon Den is its own building queue slot and should never sit idle once you have unlocked it.

Dragon Skills

Dragon Skills are the heart of the Dragon System's power. While your Dragon's level determines how many skill slots you can access and how high individual skills can be upgraded, the skill choices you make determine what type of power you gain. A badly allocated Dragon at Level 25 can be weaker than a well-focused Dragon at Level 18. Skill strategy is everything.

The Five Skill Trees

Dragon Skills are organized into five distinct trees, each serving a different strategic purpose:

1. Attack Tree

The Attack Tree provides direct offensive bonuses to your troops' damage output. Skills in this tree are further divided by troop type:

  • Infantry Attack Dragon Skill: Increases the Attack stat of all Infantry troops in your march. Available from Dragon Level 3.
  • Cavalry Attack Dragon Skill: Increases Cavalry Attack. Unlocks at Dragon Level 5.
  • Ranged Attack Dragon Skill: Increases Ranged Attack. Unlocks at Dragon Level 5.
  • All-Troops Attack Dragon Skill: A universal attack buff that applies to Infantry, Cavalry, and Ranged simultaneously. Much weaker per-troop-type than the dedicated skills, but available if you run mixed marches. Unlocks at Dragon Level 10.

2. Defense Tree

The Defense Tree reduces incoming damage by boosting your troops' armor and damage mitigation values. Like the Attack Tree, it has type-specific and universal variants:

  • Infantry Defense, Cavalry Defense, Ranged Defense: Type-specific damage reduction skills. Each reduces damage taken by that troop type by a percentage that scales with skill level.
  • All-Troops Defense: A smaller universal defense buff. More useful for mixed-march commanders than pure-type specialists.

3. HP Tree

The HP Tree increases the maximum hit points of your troops, making them harder to kill. HP is often undervalued by new players but is critically important for defenders and for enduring long rally battles. Higher HP means more wounded troops (rather than killed) when you take losses, which directly reduces your long-term resource cost.

  • Infantry HP, Cavalry HP, Ranged HP: Type-specific HP increases. Each level adds a percentage boost to the base HP of that troop type.
  • All-Troops HP: Universal HP boost — useful as a secondary investment after your primary ATK path is developed.

4. Speed Tree

The Speed Tree provides March Speed bonuses, reducing the travel time of all your marches: attacking, gathering, reinforcing, and scouting. This tree is typically lowest priority for war-focused players but is highly valued by dedicated gatherers and alliance support players who need fast reinforcement capability.

  • March Speed I–V: Each tier adds a flat percentage reduction to march travel time. Maximum investment across all five tiers can reduce march time by 25–30%.
  • Gathering Speed: Specifically boosts the speed of resource gathering marches. A niche but powerful utility skill for economic-focused commanders.

5. Economy Tree

The Economy Tree provides resource-related bonuses: increased gathering yield, reduced troop training costs, and training speed improvements. This tree is explicitly for players who prioritize castle development over combat. War-focused players should treat this tree as the absolute last priority after all combat skill paths are developed.

  • Gathering Bonus: Increases the resources collected from tiles per march.
  • Training Cost Reduction: Reduces the food and resource cost of training troops.
  • Training Speed: Stacks with Academy-based training speed research for faster troop production.

Which Skills to Prioritize: War vs. Development

War-focused commanders should follow this skill priority order without deviation:

  1. Primary Troop Type Attack (Infantry OR Cavalry OR Ranged — pick one and max it)
  2. Primary Troop Type Defense
  3. Primary Troop Type HP
  4. Secondary troop type Attack (if you run a secondary troop type in marches)
  5. All-Troops Attack (only after primary is fully developed)
  6. March Speed (useful quality-of-life, never first priority)

Development-focused commanders who primarily gather and support their alliance can modify this order:

  1. Primary Troop Type Attack (still needed — you will eventually fight)
  2. March Speed I–III (reduces time away from castle while gathering)
  3. Gathering Speed
  4. Primary Troop Type Defense
  5. Economy skills as luxury investment
Commander's Tip — Focus One Troop Type First: The single most important skill decision you will make is committing to one troop type's Attack skill and maxing it before touching any other Attack skill. A maxed Infantry Attack Dragon Skill is three to four times more powerful than the same shards spread across Infantry, Cavalry, and Ranged Attack equally. Pick your main troop type on day one and do not waver. Infantry is the most forgiving choice for new players; Cavalry provides superior speed for experienced tacticians.

Skill Upgrade Costs and Tiers

Each Dragon Skill has five upgrade tiers (I through V). The shard cost increases significantly at each tier:

  • Tier I: 20 Standard Dragon Shards — provides the baseline bonus
  • Tier II: 50 Standard Dragon Shards — roughly doubles the bonus from Tier I
  • Tier III: 120 Standard Dragon Shards — requires Dragon Level 10+ for most skills
  • Tier IV: 250 Standard Dragon Shards + 30 Elemental Shards — major power spike
  • Tier V (Max): 500 Standard Dragon Shards + 80 Elemental Shards — the endgame target

Total cost to max a single skill from Tier I to Tier V: approximately 940 Standard Shards + 110 Elemental Shards. Plan your shard farming accordingly before committing to Tier IV and V.

Dragon Shards — Sources & Farming

Dragon Shards are the limiting factor in Dragon System progression. Unlike resources that can be gathered from tiles, Shards come exclusively from specific in-game activities — most of which have daily or weekly caps. Understanding every source and maximizing your daily Shard income is the defining habit of serious Dragonia commanders.

Primary Shard Sources

1. Daily Dragon Raids

Dragon Raids are the single largest recurring Shard source for the average player. Each day, you receive 3 free Dragon Raid attempts that refresh at the daily reset (typically midnight server time). Each completed raid rewards a tiered Shard drop based on your damage contribution:

  • Bronze Contribution: 5–10 Standard Dragon Shards
  • Silver Contribution: 15–25 Standard Dragon Shards
  • Gold Contribution: 30–50 Standard Dragon Shards
  • MVP Contribution (highest damage): 50–80 Standard Shards + 1–3 Elemental Shards

A player who consistently achieves Gold or MVP tier on all 3 daily raids can accumulate 90–240 Standard Shards per day — roughly 2,700–7,200 per month purely from this source. Never miss your daily raids.

2. Alliance Dragon Events

Alliance Dragon Events are coordinated alliance-wide battles against a massive Dragon Boss. These events occur 2–3 times per week in most alliances and reward all contributing members with Shards proportional to their damage contribution. Alliance Dragon Events award Elemental Shards in addition to Standard Shards, making them the primary source of the Elemental Shards needed for Tier IV and Tier V skill upgrades.

  • Minimum contribution reward: 10–20 Elemental Shards + 20–30 Standard Shards
  • High contribution reward: 40–80 Elemental Shards + 60–100 Standard Shards
  • Top 3 contributors: Bonus chests containing Elemental Shards and Dragon EXP items

3. Weekly Tasks and Missions

The weekly task board includes rotating Dragon-specific objectives that reward Shards upon completion. These tasks typically include:

  • Complete X Dragon Raids this week — reward: 50–150 Standard Shards
  • Deal X total damage in Dragon Raids — reward: 30–80 Standard Shards
  • Participate in X Alliance Dragon Events — reward: 20–50 Elemental Shards
  • Upgrade a Dragon Skill — reward: 40 Standard Shards (one-time per upgrade, resets weekly)

Completing all Dragon weekly tasks adds approximately 140–320 Standard Shards and 20–50 Elemental Shards per week. That is a meaningful 20–40% increase to your baseline raid income.

4. VIP Rewards

VIP Level 5 and above unlocks daily VIP chest drops that include Dragon Shards as a possible reward. The drop rate scales with VIP level:

  • VIP 5–7: Small chance of 5–15 Standard Shards per day from the VIP chest
  • VIP 8–10: Guaranteed 10–20 Standard Shards per day
  • VIP 11+: Guaranteed 20–30 Standard Shards per day + occasional Elemental Shard drops

5. Special Events and Season Passes

Limited-time events are irregular but often the richest Shard sources available. Common event types that reward Shards include:

  • Dragon Festival Events: Dedicated Dragonia events with Shard reward tracks — can yield 500–2,000 Shards over 7 days if all milestones are completed
  • Season Pass (Battle Pass): The seasonal progression pass almost always includes Dragon Shards in its reward track — typically 300–600 Standard Shards across the full pass
  • Alliance Contribution Events: Alliance events where Shard donation or Dragon Raid participation contributes to alliance milestone rewards that return Shards to all members
  • Daily Login Bonus: Certain days in the monthly login calendar reward Dragon Shards — typically Days 7, 14, and 28

6. In-Game Shop (Limited)

Dragon Shard bundles are available in the in-game shop, typically at a significant gem cost. These are not recommended for F2P players. The one exception is the Daily Dragon Deal — a discounted Shard bundle that refreshes daily and typically costs 50–100 gems for 30–50 Standard Shards. If you are sitting on unspent gems with no better use, the Daily Dragon Deal is an efficient conversion.

Daily Shard Quota — What to Aim For

An optimized F2P player who completes all available Shard activities should be accumulating approximately:

  • Minimum (completing raids only): 90–140 Standard Shards per day
  • Optimized (raids + weekly tasks + VIP + events): 150–280 Standard Shards per day
  • Elemental Shards: 6–15 Elemental Shards per day on average (highly event-dependent)
Shard Stockpiling Strategy: Do not spend Shards the moment you earn them. Save Standard Shards until you have enough for the next skill tier you are targeting. Spending 20 Shards on Tier I when you are 80 away from Tier II is fine, but spending into a second skill tree while your primary skill is at Tier III is a major strategic error — you are diluting your power gain significantly.

Dragon Raids

Dragon Raids are the primary gameplay activity of the Dragon System — a dedicated combat mode where commanders send their troops against Dragon Bosses to earn Shards, EXP, and glory. Raids come in two fundamentally different formats: Solo Raids and Alliance Dragon Battles, each with distinct mechanics and optimal strategies.

Solo Dragon Raids

Solo Raids are the daily bread-and-butter Shard farming activity. You launch a march of troops against a Dragon Boss that only you are fighting. The mechanics are straightforward but reward optimization:

  • Access: Tap the Dragon Raid icon in your Dragon Den or from the Dragon tab in the Activity menu.
  • Troop Deployment: Send your strongest available troops — the Dragon Boss has fixed stats for each difficulty tier, and your march power determines whether you reach Gold/MVP threshold.
  • Boss HP: Solo Raid bosses have tiered HP pools based on the Raid Difficulty you select (Normal, Hard, Heroic). Higher difficulties award more Shards but require stronger marches.
  • Battle Resolution: Battles are auto-resolved. You do not need to watch or interact — march out, come back, collect rewards.
  • Troop Losses: Solo Raids result in troop wounds, not deaths. All wounded troops from Dragon Raids automatically recover in the Hospital at no additional cost, making Raids completely free to engage with at full power every day.

Solo Raid Difficulty Tiers

Select the highest difficulty your march power can achieve a Gold-tier reward at. Do not play Normal difficulty for easy wins — the Shard delta between Normal Gold and Heroic Gold is substantial enough to make the difficulty increase mandatory for serious progression:

  • Normal Mode Boss HP: 500,000 — Minimum march power: ~50,000. Gold reward: 20–30 Standard Shards per raid.
  • Hard Mode Boss HP: 2,000,000 — Minimum march power: ~200,000. Gold reward: 35–50 Standard Shards per raid.
  • Heroic Mode Boss HP: 8,000,000 — Minimum march power: ~800,000. Gold reward: 50–80 Standard Shards per raid.
  • Legendary Mode Boss HP: 25,000,000 — Minimum march power: ~2,000,000. Gold reward: 80–120 Standard Shards per raid. Unlocked at Dragon Level 15+.

Alliance Dragon Battles

Alliance Dragon Battles are coordinated events initiated by Alliance Officers or the Alliance Leader. A designated Dragon Boss with an enormous HP pool spawns, and all alliance members who participate within the event window deal damage and earn rewards based on their contribution. These battles are fundamentally different from Solo Raids in three important ways:

Boss HP Tiers in Alliance Dragon Battles

Alliance Dragon Boss HP scales with your alliance's total power and participation history. Most mid-tier alliances encounter bosses with:

  • Tier 1 Alliance Boss: 50,000,000 HP — accessible to alliances with 20–30 active members
  • Tier 2 Alliance Boss: 200,000,000 HP — rewards significantly better Elemental Shard drops
  • Tier 3 Alliance Boss: 800,000,000 HP — top-tier bosses for large, powerful alliances; rewards best-in-game Elemental Shard bundles

Damage Contribution Mechanics

Your reward tier in Alliance Dragon Battles is determined by your percentage of total damage dealt to the boss, not your raw damage number. This means:

  • In a small, weak alliance fighting a Tier 1 boss, even modest marches can reach Gold tier.
  • In a powerful alliance fighting a Tier 3 boss, you need to send multiple marches at maximum power to reach Gold tier.
  • Rally Participation: If your Alliance Leader or an R4 opens a Dragon Rally (a coordinated multi-march attack), joining the rally counts your troops' damage individually toward your personal contribution score. Always join Dragon Rallies.

Reward Tiers in Alliance Dragon Battles

  • Participant Tier (any damage): 5–15 Standard Shards + 2–5 Elemental Shards
  • Contributor Tier (top 50% damage): 20–40 Standard Shards + 10–20 Elemental Shards
  • Gold Tier (top 20% damage): 50–80 Standard Shards + 25–40 Elemental Shards + bonus Dragon EXP chest
  • Top 3 Damage Dealers: Additional Named Reward Chest — contains 50–100 Elemental Shards and rare Dragon Shard Packs
Raid Timing Tip: Alliance Dragon Battles have a time limit of 2–4 hours from spawn. Set an alarm or notification so you never miss the window. Missing an Alliance Dragon Battle is the equivalent of losing 3–5 days of Solo Raid Shard income in a single event slot.

Dragon Raid Strategies for Maximum Reward

  • Always send your full-power march: Since all troop losses are wounds (not deaths) and auto-heal for free, there is zero reason to hold back. Send your strongest troops every single raid.
  • Use Dragon Active Skills during raids: Your Dragon's Active Skills deal bonus damage during raid battles. Activate them manually from the battle screen if your Dragon is at a level that unlocks active abilities.
  • Match troop type to boss vulnerability: Dragon Bosses in Heroic+ difficulty have type-specific resistances. Check the Boss Info panel before launching your march to see which troop type deals bonus damage this rotation.
  • Stack Attack Research before Raid Season: If a Dragon Festival or limited Raid Event is coming, prioritize spending Research speedups on Attack nodes beforehand so your damage output is maximized when Shard rewards are at their peak.

Leveling Your Dragon

Dragon leveling is the backbone of your long-term Dragonia investment. Each level increases your Dragon's base stats and unlocks access to higher skill tiers. Understanding the EXP system and the specific milestones that matter most will help you plan your resource allocation and set realistic monthly targets.

Dragon EXP Sources

Dragon EXP is earned from multiple sources, most of which overlap with your Shard farming activities:

  • Dragon Shard Conversion: Every Dragon Shard you use on a skill upgrade contributes a small amount of EXP to your Dragon's level progress. This is the passive EXP gain that comes naturally from your skill investment.
  • Dragon EXP Potions: Dedicated EXP items dropped from Alliance Dragon Battles, Dragon Festival Events, and Gold-tier Solo Raid rewards. These can be banked and used strategically when you are close to a milestone level.
  • Daily Dragon Tasks: Completing the three Daily Dragon Tasks (one for completing raids, one for dealing damage, one for logging into the Dragon interface) each rewards a small EXP packet — roughly 500–1,000 EXP per task.
  • Alliance Dragon EXP Chests: Special chests from Alliance Dragon Battles contain EXP in addition to Shards. Top contributors receive these chests as bonus rewards.

Milestone Levels and What They Unlock

Dragon leveling is not a smooth linear progression — there are specific milestones where major new content or capabilities unlock. Target these milestones in sequence as your primary leveling goals:

Level 5 — First Active Skill Slot

At Dragon Level 5, your first Active Battle Skill slot unlocks. This allows you to equip one active Dragon ability that can be manually triggered during combat (or automatically triggers in some combat scenarios). The Active Skills available at this tier are modest but represent a meaningful free damage or buff source. Priority: equip the Active Attack Skill to boost your raid damage immediately.

Level 10 — Second Skill Tree Unlocked + All-Troops Skills

Dragon Level 10 is the first major milestone and the target all new players should sprint toward. At Level 10:

  • The second passive skill slot becomes available — you can now invest in a Defense skill alongside your primary Attack skill
  • All-Troops Attack and Defense skills become available for the first time
  • Heroic Raid difficulty unlocks if your march power meets the threshold
  • Your Dragon's visual appearance upgrades to the Juvenile stage — a cosmetic but satisfying milestone
  • Dragon passive buffs are now high enough to be visible in battle outcomes — players at this level consistently outperform those stuck below Level 5 in direct PvP

Level 15 — Third Skill Slot + HP Skills Unlock

At Level 15, the HP skill tree becomes fully available and a third passive skill slot opens. This is the point where you should begin investing Shards into your primary troop type's HP skill (after Attack is at Tier III and Defense is at Tier II). Level 15 also unlocks Dragon Skill Respec — a costly but available option to redistribute skill points if you previously made poor allocation decisions.

Level 20 — Second Active Skill + Speed Skills

Dragon Level 20 is the second major milestone and a significant power spike:

  • Second Active Skill slot unlocks — you can now equip two active Dragon abilities simultaneously
  • March Speed and Economy skill trees become fully accessible
  • Dragon visual upgrades to Adult stage
  • Passive buff totals at this level (with a focused skill path) are comparable to a full secondary research tree in the Military tab
  • Legendary Raid difficulty unlocks at Level 20 — the highest tier of Solo Raids with the best Shard returns

Level 25 — Full Skill Access + Enhanced Active Skills

At Level 25, all skill trees are fully unlocked at their maximum tier potential. Active Skills can be upgraded beyond their base versions using Elemental Shards, providing significantly more powerful burst effects. Players at Level 25 with a maxed primary Attack skill are tier-1 threats in kingdom PvP.

Level 30 — Elder Dragon + Dragon Aura

The ultimate Dragon milestone. At Level 30, your Dragon achieves the Elder Dragon form and unlocks the exclusive Dragon Aura ability — a persistent passive effect that radiates a battlefield presence bonus to all allied troops within a certain battle range. Dragon Aura effects include:

  • +10% All-Troops ATK for every march in a rally you participate in
  • +5% damage reduction for your castle in garrison during castle defense battles
  • A unique cosmetic Dragon Aura visual effect visible to all players on the world map
Critical Warning — Skill Spreading: The most damaging mistake you can make in Dragon progression is spreading your Dragon Shards across multiple troop type skills simultaneously. A player who splits shards equally between Infantry Attack, Cavalry Attack, and Ranged Attack will have three Tier I skills by the time a focused player has one Tier III skill. The focused player's single Tier III skill provides more raw attack power than all three Tier I skills combined. Commit to one troop type. Period. Only diversify your skill investment after your primary ATK skill reaches Tier IV at minimum.

Dragon in Battle

Understanding how your Dragon interacts with the battle system is what separates a player who has a Dragon from a player who truly uses a Dragon. The passive buffs are automatic — they apply whenever your Dragon is assigned to a march — but the active skill system requires deliberate management, and the synergy between your Dragon, your Hero, and your troop composition creates combinations that multiply your effective combat power.

Assigning Your Dragon to a March

Your Dragon must be explicitly assigned to a march to provide its passive buffs. This is done through the march formation screen before sending troops:

  1. Open the March Formation screen (tap "March" then "Customize March")
  2. Tap the Dragon slot at the top of the formation panel
  3. Select your Dragon — only one Dragon can be assigned per march
  4. Confirm. Your Dragon's passive bonuses now apply to this specific march

Your Dragon can only be assigned to one march at a time. If you have multiple active marches, only the one with your Dragon benefits from Dragon passive skills. Choose wisely: assign your Dragon to your highest-priority march (typically your main PvP or rally march, not a gathering march).

Activating Dragon Active Skills in Battle

Active Skills are manually triggered from the Battle Screen during live combat. The interface appears as a hotbar at the bottom of the screen during active battles:

  • Skill Cooldown: Each Active Skill has a cooldown timer (typically 30–60 seconds between uses). Use the first activation immediately when battle begins, then monitor the cooldown for your second use.
  • Skill Types Available: Burst Damage, Temporary ATK Boost, Temporary DEF Shield, and Healing Pulse are the most common Active Skill types in the Dragon System. Burst Damage and ATK Boost are highest priority for offensive players.
  • Timing for Maximum Impact: ATK Boost Active Skills should be triggered at the moment the initial clash occurs — the first combat round in P&C deals a disproportionately high damage swing that ATK Boost amplifies most effectively. Burst Damage can be used at any point but is most efficient when your troop count is highest (early in the battle).

How Dragon Buffs Stack with Hero Skills

Dragon passive buffs and Hero combat skills are additive with each other — meaning they add together before being applied to your base troop stats. The combined total is then multiplied by your Research and Gear bonuses. The full stacking formula is:

Effective ATK = Base ATK × (1 + Dragon ATK% + Hero ATK% + Gear ATK%) × (1 + Research ATK%)

This means that Dragon ATK skills and Hero ATK skills add together in the first multiplier bracket, while Research ATK is in its own separate multiplier. This is why both Dragon and Research investments are critically important — they multiplicatively amplify each other, not just stack additively.

Practical Example

Commander with Infantry Cavalry at 10,000 base ATK:

  • Dragon Infantry ATK Skill (Tier III): +25%
  • Hero Combat Skill (Infantry ATK): +20%
  • Gear (Infantry ATK Set): +15%
  • Research (Infantry ATK): +40%

Effective ATK = 10,000 × (1 + 0.25 + 0.20 + 0.15) × (1 + 0.40) = 10,000 × 1.60 × 1.40 = 22,400 effective ATK

Without the Dragon's +25% from Tier III skill, that drops to: 10,000 × 1.35 × 1.40 = 18,900 effective ATK — a 18.5% reduction in effective power from a single Dragon Skill difference.

Troop Type Synergy with Dragon Skills

The most powerful Dragon setups pair the Dragon's primary skill investment with both your Hero's skill tree and your research tree, all pointing to the same troop type:

  • Full Infantry Synergy: Infantry ATK Dragon Skill (Tier III+) + Hero with Infantry Talent Path + Infantry ATK Research nodes. This setup is the highest single-march power achievable by F2P players.
  • Full Cavalry Synergy: Cavalry ATK Dragon Skill + Cavalry-specialized Hero + Cavalry Research. Cavalry marches benefit additionally from March Speed synergy — faster marches mean more PvP engagements per hour, multiplying the value of the ATK investment.
  • Full Ranged Synergy: Ranged ATK Dragon Skill + Ranged Hero + Ranged Research. Ranged builds provide the highest sustained damage against garrison defenses, making them ideal for siege warfare.

Dragon in Rally Battles

When joining an Alliance Rally as a participant (not the rally captain), your Dragon's passive skills still apply to your contributed troops. The Rally Captain's Dragon also applies its skills to the full rally march. This means in an optimally coordinated rally, two Dragon buff sets are active simultaneously — the Captain's Dragon and your Dragon. For maximum rally power, coordinate with your alliance to ensure the Rally Captain always has the highest-level Dragon assigned.

Battle Intelligence: Always check the enemy's Dragon status before committing to a PvP attack. The Scout Report shows the target's Dragon Level and active Dragon Skill tier. A player with a Dragon Level 20+ and maxed Attack skills requires a 25–35% larger march to achieve a comparable outcome to fighting an undragonized target. Factor this into your march power calculations.

F2P Dragon Strategy

Free-to-play commanders face a slower Dragon progression curve than paying players, but the Dragon System is one of the most F2P-friendly in Puzzles & Conquest. Daily Raid rewards, weekly tasks, and Alliance Dragon Events provide a steady Shard income that, managed correctly, delivers meaningful Dragon growth every single month. The key for F2P players is discipline: do not deviate from the optimal path, and do not rush upgrades that are not on the critical sequence.

Monthly F2P Dragon Milestones

Based on consistent F2P activity (all daily raids, weekly tasks completed, active in at least 2 Alliance Dragon Events per week), here is what realistic Dragon progression looks like:

  • Month 1: Dragon unlocked at HQ 15, Dragon Den built, Dragon reaches Level 5–7, Primary ATK Skill unlocked at Tier I–II. Goal: establish the daily Raid habit and never miss a reset.
  • Month 2: Dragon reaches Level 8–10, Primary ATK Skill upgraded to Tier II–III. Begin saving Elemental Shards from Alliance Dragon Events without spending them yet. Hit the Level 10 milestone by end of month.
  • Month 3: Dragon reaches Level 10–13, Primary ATK Skill at Tier III. Unlock and invest in primary troop type Defense Skill to Tier I. Dragon Den upgraded to Level 4–5 to allow progression past Level 10.
  • Month 4: Dragon reaches Level 13–16, ATK Skill at Tier III–IV (if Elemental Shard stockpile permits). Begin HP Skill investment at Tier I. Push Dragon Den to Level 6.
  • Month 5–6: Dragon reaches Level 16–20 with ATK Skill at Tier IV, Defense Skill at Tier II, HP Skill at Tier I. Hit the Level 20 milestone. This is where F2P players start feeling genuinely competitive in alliance PvP.
  • Months 7–12: Slow push to Level 20–25 with skill refinement. ATK Skill targeted to Tier V (maximum) by month 10 for focused players. Level 25 by month 12 is achievable for highly active F2P commanders.

The F2P Priority Order (Non-Negotiable)

Follow this sequence without deviation:

  1. Unlock Dragon — HQ 15, Dragon Den built, Dragon Egg claimed
  2. Dragon Level 10 — The first major power milestone. All Shard income goes to EXP conversion until this is reached.
  3. Primary ATK Skill to Tier III — After hitting Level 10, immediately begin the ATK skill investment path. Tier III is the inflection point where the bonus becomes combat-changing.
  4. Dragon Level 20 — The second major milestone. Begin this push after ATK Skill hits Tier III.
  5. Primary ATK Skill to Tier V (Max) — The endgame primary goal. Everything before this is preparation.
  6. Primary DEF Skill to Tier III — After ATK is maxed, begin Defense investment.
  7. Primary HP Skill to Tier II–III — Third investment layer.
  8. Dragon Level 25–30 — The final long-term level targets.
  9. Secondary skill trees — Only after all primary troop type combat skills are at Tier III+.

Event Timing for F2P Maximization

F2P commanders should stockpile EXP Potions and Dragon Shard Packs before Dragon Festival Events. During Festival Events, many Shard-spending activities grant bonus rewards or contribute to event milestone tracks that return Shards. Spending your stockpile during an event rather than between events effectively gives you 20–40% more value per shard due to event bonuses. Check the Event Calendar and plan major Dragon investments around Festival events.

F2P Habit Stack: Log in during three specific windows every day — morning (daily reset for Raid attempts), midday (check for Alliance Dragon Event timers), evening (complete any Daily Dragon Tasks before they reset). This three-window habit ensures you never miss Shard income and captures the highest-value daily rewards consistently.

Alliance Selection Matters for F2P

Your alliance quality directly impacts your Dragon System progression as a F2P player. An active alliance running Tier 2–3 Alliance Dragon Events rewards 3–5x more Elemental Shards per week than a quiet alliance running Tier 1 events. If your current alliance runs fewer than 2 Dragon Events per week, consider migrating to a more active alliance — the Elemental Shard income difference alone will accelerate your Dragon to Level 20 weeks or months faster.

Common Dragon Mistakes

The Dragon System has a deceptively simple interface that masks significant strategic depth. Most players make the same set of mistakes during their early Dragon progression — and these mistakes compound over time, creating a power gap that takes months to recover from. Study these common errors, recognize whether you are making any of them, and correct course immediately.

Mistake 1: Spreading Skills Across Multiple Troop Types

This is the single most damaging mistake in the Dragon System. New players see three Attack skills (Infantry, Cavalry, Ranged) and split their Shards evenly across all three, thinking they are building a "balanced" dragon. This is catastrophically inefficient.

  • The math against spreading: Three Tier I skills cost 60 Standard Shards total. One Tier II skill costs 50 Shards and provides roughly 2.5x the bonus of a Tier I skill for that troop type. The "balanced" player gets 3 × 5% = 15% total attack across three troop types. The focused player gets one 12% buff for their main troop — nearly as much to their primary troop type from spending less.
  • The compounding problem: This mistake gets worse at higher tiers. Spreading means your highest skill is always one tier behind the focused player's skill. At Tier III and above, the gap in raw bonus values is enormous.
  • The fix: Identify your primary troop type (Infantry for beginners; Cavalry for experienced players who want speed; Ranged for siege specialists). Every Attack shard goes to that one skill until it is maxed at Tier V. No exceptions.

Mistake 2: Ignoring Daily Dragon Raids

Missing daily Dragon Raids is the equivalent of voluntarily skipping your daily income. Each missed raid represents 30–80 Standard Shards forfeited — permanently. There are no make-up raids, no rollover raid attempts. The three daily attempts expire at reset and are gone forever.

  • The real cost of skipping: A player who misses one raid per day for a month has given up 900–2,400 Shards — the equivalent of a full Tier III skill upgrade or a significant chunk of Dragon EXP progress.
  • The fix: Set a phone alarm for 15 minutes before your server's daily reset. Use those 15 minutes to complete all three daily raids before the reset, and then complete them again immediately after the reset refreshes your attempts. This "double dip" window is the most efficient raid habit.

Mistake 3: Wasting Shards on Low-Priority Skills

The Economy and Speed skill trees look appealing because they provide visible quality-of-life benefits (faster marches, more resources). But investing Shards in these trees before your combat skills are fully developed is a strategic error that costs you combat power for months.

  • Why it is a trap: March Speed from the Dragon Speed tree saves you a few minutes per march. At the same shard cost, a higher ATK Skill tier permanently increases your damage by a significant percentage in every battle for the rest of the game. The ATK upgrade is infinitely more valuable.
  • The correct prioritization: Primary ATK → Primary DEF → Primary HP → March Speed (only if you are a dedicated support/gatherer) → Economy tree (only in retirement, when everything else is maxed)

Mistake 4: Not Upgrading the Dragon Den Proactively

Players often ignore the Dragon Den building queue and focus only on direct castle buildings and research. This creates a ceiling problem: when your Dragon's EXP fills up against the level cap imposed by the Dragon Den, all additional EXP is wasted. You literally cannot progress until the Den upgrades.

  • The fix: Always have a Dragon Den upgrade queued when one is available. The Den uses its own building slot in most server configurations and does not compete with your main castle upgrade queue. There is no reason not to have it running continuously.

Mistake 5: Not Assigning the Dragon to the Right March

Forgetting to assign your Dragon to your primary combat march — or worse, leaving it on a gathering march during a PvP battle — nullifies all of your Dragon investment for that battle. This is a mechanical mistake that is easy to make when you are managing multiple marches quickly.

  • The fix: Make Dragon assignment part of your pre-march checklist. Before every PvP march: Hero assigned? Dragon assigned? Skills equipped? Formation set? Build this four-point check into muscle memory.

Mistake 6: Using Dragon EXP Potions Carelessly

Dragon EXP Potions should be used strategically — specifically to hit milestone levels (Level 10, Level 20) sooner, or to reach a level that unlocks a new skill slot you are ready to invest in. Spending EXP Potions randomly instead of saving them for these milestone pushes costs you weeks of effective progress.

  • The fix: Bank Dragon EXP Potions until you are within 10–20% of a milestone level, then use them to complete the milestone. The unlocks at milestone levels (new skill slots, new difficulties, etc.) compound your future Shard and EXP income — getting there faster has compounding benefits.

Mistake 7: Skipping Alliance Dragon Events

Alliance Dragon Events are the primary source of Elemental Shards, which are required for Tier IV and Tier V skill upgrades. Players who consistently skip these events find themselves with a soft ceiling at Tier III — unable to progress further because they lack Elemental Shards, even if they have plenty of Standard Shards.

The Elemental Shard Bottleneck: Tier IV and Tier V upgrades require 30 and 80 Elemental Shards respectively per skill. If you participate in zero Alliance Dragon Events for two months, you will have exactly zero Elemental Shards — making it completely impossible to advance your primary ATK skill past Tier III regardless of your Standard Shard stockpile. Alliance participation is not optional for Dragon progression.

Mistake 8: Respeccing Skills Too Early

The Dragon Skill Respec feature (available from Dragon Level 15) allows you to redistribute your invested skill points, but it costs a significant amount of Elemental Shards and/or premium currency to execute. Players who switch troop types mid-game and respec their Dragon are effectively paying double for the same skill tier — once to reach the original skill level and once to pay the respec cost. Choose your troop type commitment on day one and stick to it.

RESEARCH PRIORITY GUIDE

The definitive order to unlock research — maximize buffs and minimize wasted time

Why Research is Your Most Important Investment

In Puzzles & Conquest, many systems compete for your resources and attention — gear, heroes, buildings, alliances. Of all of them, Research is the single highest-return investment you can make for your long-term power level. Understanding why is the first step to prioritizing it correctly.

Research provides permanent, compounding stat buffs that cannot be removed or debuffed. Unlike gear that can be destroyed during siege, or hero skills that only activate under specific conditions, your research bonuses are always active — in attack, in defense, during gathering, every single second of every day. They are baked into your base stats and they never go away.

The compounding nature of research is what makes it so powerful. Early nodes unlock later nodes. Speed bonuses from early research reduce the time cost of all subsequent research. A player who prioritizes Research Speed nodes in their first month will complete the same research tree in 30–40% less total time than a player who ignores those nodes. That time advantage persists and grows with every subsequent unlock.

The Power Gap Is Real

The difference between a player with maxed research in their primary troop type versus a player who neglected their tech trees can be 200–500% in effective troop stats. That is not a typo. A fully researched Infantry player might have 180% Infantry Attack from research nodes alone before any hero skills, gear, or alliance tech are factored in. A player who skipped the Military tree might have 20–40%.

This gap is what makes research the great equalizer between free-to-play and paying players. A F2P commander who spends 12 months consistently building their research trees will statistically outperform a heavy spender who neglects their tech trees. The spender can accelerate with speedups, but if they invest those speedups in the wrong order — or in the wrong trees — the advantage evaporates.

Commander's Insight: If you do nothing else after reading this guide, do this — unlock the Research Speed nodes in the Development Tree as your absolute first priority. Everything that comes after will be faster because of it.

Research is also immune to being countered. Your enemy cannot debuff your research. Alliance enemies cannot strip it. It does not require activation or cooldown management. It is pure, passive, permanent power — and that makes it the foundation of everything else.

The Two Trees: Development vs Military

The research system in Puzzles & Conquest is divided into two fundamentally different tech trees, each serving a distinct strategic purpose. Understanding the difference — and more importantly, the order in which to pursue them — is the core of this guide.

Development Tree

The Development Tree covers all economic and infrastructure upgrades. Its nodes boost the speed and efficiency of every core castle operation:

  • Building Speed Boost: Reduces construction time for all buildings by cumulative percentages.
  • Research Speed Boost: Directly reduces the time required for all future research — the highest-ROI nodes in the game.
  • Training Speed & Cost Reduction: Faster and cheaper troop production keeps your army topped off continuously.
  • Gathering Speed & Load Increase: More resources per march, faster collection from tiles.
  • March Speed: All marches move faster — gathering, attacking, and reinforcing.
  • Training Capacity: More troops produced per batch, reducing the number of manual queue operations needed.

Military Tree

The Military Tree covers all combat stat upgrades for your three troop types — Infantry, Cavalry, and Ranged. Its nodes provide:

  • Troop Attack by Type: Flat and percentage attack increases for Infantry, Cavalry, or Ranged independently.
  • Troop Defense by Type: Damage reduction and armor boosts for each troop type.
  • Troop Health by Type: HP increases that directly affect how long your troops survive in battle.
  • March Size: The number of troops you can send in a single march or rally.
  • Rally Capacity: The maximum troops that can join a rally you or your alliance launches.
  • Casualty Reduction: Percentage of killed troops that are converted to wounded instead of lost permanently.

The Core Rule: Development First

New players consistently make the mistake of rushing into the Military Tree, eager to boost their troop stats and start hitting. This is a trap. The correct strategy is clear and non-negotiable:

The Golden Rule: Start the Development Tree first — specifically Research Speed, Building Speed, and Training Speed nodes — then pivot 100% to the Military Tree for your chosen troop type. The speed boosts compound every future upgrade. Skipping them costs you months of real time.

The math is straightforward. If your current research speed gives you a 12-day timer for a Military Tree node, and you first unlock Research Speed I–III (which takes perhaps 4 days total), those three speed nodes might reduce that Military node to 8 days. You "lost" 4 days getting the speed nodes, but every subsequent node — dozens of them — is now 33% faster. The break-even point is reached within 3–4 nodes. After that, you are perpetually ahead.

Development Tree Priority Order

Your Development Tree research should follow a phased approach tied directly to your Headquarters level. Each phase has a clear objective and a defined stopping point where you pivot to Military research.

Phase 1 — HQ Levels 1 to 15: The Foundation Sprint

This is the most critical phase of your entire research career. Every node you unlock here accelerates all future research, making this the period where the game rewards you most for playing smart. Work through these nodes in this exact order:

  1. Advanced Construction — Reduces ALL building time by 20%. This is the single highest-priority node in the entire Development Tree. Unlock it before anything else. With HQ upgrades gating your research progress, reducing building time directly reduces how long you wait between research unlocks.
  2. Building Speed Boost I, II, III — Follow Advanced Construction immediately. Stack these to dramatically reduce construction times throughout Phase 1 and Phase 2.
  3. Research Speed Boost I, II — Unlock as soon as you have Building Speed I–II. These nodes reduce the timer on every single research node from this point forward. Their ROI grows with every subsequent unlock.
  4. Training Speed I — Your army needs to grow while your research runs. Training Speed I ensures your barracks stay productive during the long overnight research timers.
  5. Training Cost Reduction I, II — Cheaper troops mean more troops with the same resources. Stack these early so your training efficiency compounds as your capacity grows.
Warning: Do NOT research any Military Tree nodes during Phase 1. The temptation is real — troop stats seem directly valuable. Resist it. Your Phase 1 window is short and every node you spend outside of Development during this phase delays the speed compounding that benefits you for the next 12 months.

Phase 2 — HQ Levels 15 to 20: Economic Maturity

By HQ 15, your Development Tree foundation is in place. Now expand into the economic quality-of-life nodes that support your Military pivot:

  • Gathering Speed — Faster resource collection from tiles means more resources to fuel Military research and troop training simultaneously.
  • Load Increase — More resources per gather march reduces the number of trips required to sustain your economy. Pairs directly with Gathering Speed.
  • March Speed — All marches move faster, including attack and reinforcement marches. This has combat applications as well as economic ones.
  • Training Capacity — Larger batch sizes mean less manual queue management and more troops produced during offline periods.

Phase 3 — HQ 20+: Prerequisite Cleanup

At HQ 20, the highest-tier Military Tree nodes become accessible, but many require Development Tree prerequisites. Complete all remaining Development nodes that unlock Military prerequisites. Do not research Development nodes that have no Military prerequisite value — defer those indefinitely.

Timeline Note: For a daily-active player using the Production Talent Set during research, Phase 1 takes roughly 3–5 weeks, Phase 2 another 3–4 weeks, and Phase 3 is completed gradually alongside Military research over months 3–4. This timeline compresses significantly with speedups or VIP-level boosts.

Military Tree: Your Troop Type First

Once you have completed Development Phase 1 and Phase 2, the time has come to commit fully to the Military Tree. This is where your permanent combat identity is established — and the choice you make here will define your playstyle for months or years.

The first decision is your primary troop type: Infantry, Cavalry, or Ranged. This choice should be made before you research a single Military node, because your research order depends entirely on it. Each type has different strengths, different hero synergies, and different strategic roles. The sections below cover each path in detail.

Universal Military Research Priority Order

Regardless of which troop type you choose, the priority order within that type is always the same:

  1. Attack Boost I through V — Attack is king in Puzzles & Conquest. Higher attack means more enemies killed per round, which means fewer counterattack opportunities and lower casualties on your side. Max Attack first, always.
  2. Health Boost I through V — Health determines how long your troops survive under fire. More health means your attack nodes continue dealing damage for more rounds before your troops die. Attack and Health together define your effective DPS.
  3. Defense Boost I through III — Defense reduces incoming damage, but its relative impact is lower than Attack and Health because most high-level combat in P&C is decided before Defense multipliers heavily affect outcomes.
  4. Troop Size Increase — Larger marches mean more troops dealing damage simultaneously. Unlock this after your per-troop stats are strong.
  5. Casualty Reduction — Reduces the percentage of killed troops lost permanently. Important for sustainability but not a combat power multiplier like Attack or Health.
Critical Rule: Never research Military nodes for a secondary troop type until your primary type's Attack and Health nodes are fully maxed to the highest tier currently available at your HQ level. Split research is the most common mistake made by intermediate players — and it results in being weaker in every troop type instead of dominant in one.

The logic is simple: being 100% maxed in one troop type makes you extremely dangerous in your preferred combat mode. Being 60% maxed in two troop types makes you mediocre in all combat modes. Commit to your primary type, dominate that lane, then branch out.

Infantry Research Path

Infantry is the backbone of any serious defensive operation in Puzzles & Conquest. Where Cavalry excels at rapid strikes and Ranged dominates burst damage, Infantry is built for sustained combat, attrition warfare, and long-term occupation of high-value tiles like Altars.

Infantry Research Node Order

  1. Infantry Attack I, II, III, IV, V — Your damage output determines the battle. Max all five tiers before moving to Health.
  2. Infantry Health I, II, III, IV, V — Infantry's defensive durability is what sets it apart. High Health means Infantry stays in the fight through multiple rounds of combat, allowing all those Attack buffs to deal damage across more turns.
  3. Shield Wall — The Infantry-specific casualty reduction node. Unlike the generic Casualty Reduction node, Shield Wall is tuned specifically for Infantry and provides a meaningfully better rate. It reduces the percentage of your Infantry that converts from Wounded to Killed after battle — directly protecting your troop investment.
  4. Infantry Defense I, II, III — Once your Attack and Health are maxed, Defense rounds out your Infantry's ability to absorb punishment in prolonged engagements.
  5. Infantry March Size — More Infantry per march means more total HP in the fight and more attack dice rolling each round.

Tactical Role

Infantry is the optimal choice for players who want to defend Altars and other key map objectives for extended periods. The combination of high Health, Shield Wall, and Defense makes Infantry the hardest troop type to dislodge from an occupied position. Alliance defenders, Altar holders, and players who prefer a reactive playstyle should choose Infantry.

Hero Synergies

  • Medusa: Her skill set amplifies Infantry defensive stats and provides debuffs that reduce enemy attack against your Infantry — an ideal pairing for defensive Altar holds.
  • Achilles: Enhances Infantry attack multipliers and provides battle initiation skills that maximize Infantry's damage in the opening rounds of combat before enemy forces can adapt.
Strategic Note: Infantry requires more time investment to show results compared to Cavalry or Ranged, because its strength is sustained over long battles rather than in opening burst damage. Commit to the full Infantry tree before evaluating your power level — the tree rewards patience.

Cavalry Research Path

Cavalry is the aggressive player's weapon of choice. When matched against Infantry, Cavalry deals the highest damage per troop in the game thanks to the type advantage multiplier. Combined with the fastest march speed of all troop types, Cavalry players can hit first, hit hard, and be gone before the enemy can respond.

Cavalry Research Node Order

  1. Cavalry Attack I, II, III, IV, V — Cavalry's primary advantage is offensive firepower. Attack nodes amplify the type-advantage multiplier against Infantry to devastating levels. These are mandatory first.
  2. Cavalry Health I, II, III, IV, V — Cavalry is faster but naturally thinner than Infantry. Health nodes compensate, keeping your Cavalry in the fight long enough to convert attack power into kills.
  3. Cavalry Speed — This is a critical node unique to Cavalry. Rally timing in competitive play is decided by seconds. Cavalry Speed reduces march travel time, which affects both how quickly you can launch surprise attacks and how quickly you can extract troops from danger. Prioritize this above Defense nodes.
  4. Cavalry March Size — More Cavalry per march multiplies every Attack and Health buff already researched. Unlock before finishing Defense tiers.
  5. Cavalry Defense I, II, III — Cavalry Defense is your last research priority because Cavalry wins by killing enemies before they can counterattack, not by tanking damage.

Tactical Role

Cavalry is ideal for aggressive players who rally, hit fast, and relocate. In Battle of Supremacy and Military Expedition events, Cavalry players dictate the tempo of combat. They choose when to engage, choose their targets, and can disengage before slow Infantry marches can respond. The high DPS against Infantry — the most common troop type in the game — makes Cavalry statistically the strongest 1v1 combat option for competitive play.

Hero Synergies

  • Atlas: His skills specifically enhance Cavalry attack and provide march size bonuses that stack multiplicatively with the Cavalry March Size research node — a powerful combination for maximum rally strength.
  • Morgana: Provides debuff skills that reduce enemy defensive stats when engaging with Cavalry, effectively amplifying your already high Cavalry Attack output by reducing the enemy's damage absorption.
Pro Tip: Cavalry Speed is often underrated by new players. In high-level alliance warfare, arriving 30 seconds before your rally window closes can mean the difference between a successful coordinated strike and a failed assault. Research Cavalry Speed before any Defense node.

Ranged Research Path

Ranged troops are the high-ceiling, high-risk option in Puzzles & Conquest. When deployed correctly against Cavalry — the troop type Ranged counters — they deliver the highest burst damage in the game. However, Ranged troops are vulnerable to Infantry, and their value diminishes significantly against a well-researched Infantry opponent.

Ranged Research Node Order

  1. Ranged Attack I, II, III, IV, V — Burst damage is the entire point of Ranged troops. Max Attack first to ensure that your advantage window — the opening rounds of combat — deals maximum damage before the enemy can adapt.
  2. Ranged Health I, II, III, IV, V — Ranged troops naturally have lower base health than Infantry, making Health nodes a priority to extend their survivability through the engagement long enough to finish off their target.
  3. Ranged Defense I, II, III — Defense is a stronger priority for Ranged than for Cavalry because Ranged troops tend to take more sustained fire during the longer engagements where they choose to operate. Defense helps them last through the burst window.
  4. Ranged Range Boost — This unique Ranged node enables your troops to attack from farther positions within the battle grid, allowing them to deal damage in rounds where enemy melee troops have not yet reached them. It effectively increases total damage output by adding free attack rounds at the start of combat.
  5. Ranged March Size — More Ranged per march amplifies all preceding Attack buffs and extends the Range Boost advantage to a larger force.

Tactical Role

Ranged is the expert-level troop choice, recommended for experienced commanders who scout carefully before engaging. The key to Ranged success is knowing your target — attacking Cavalry and avoiding Infantry. Ranged players who engage blindly will suffer heavily against Infantry defenders. But a Ranged player who picks their battles correctly, using scout intel to confirm Cavalry-heavy targets, will achieve the highest kill ratios in the game.

Hero Synergies

  • Horus: His ability set stacks directly with Ranged Attack research, providing additional percentage attack bonuses and a unique cooldown skill that resets faster when Ranged troops land the killing blow on enemy units.
  • Artemis: Enhances the Range Boost effect by extending the positional advantage further, and provides a passive that increases Ranged Health by a percentage that multiplies with your Health research nodes.
Warning: Ranged troops are extremely vulnerable to Infantry. Do not send Ranged marches to attack or reinforce positions held by Infantry. Always scout before engaging. A Ranged player who fails to scout consistently will have one of the highest casualty rates in their alliance.

Efficiency Tips: Getting More from Research

Raw research order is only half the equation. The other half is how efficiently you execute each research unlock. These tips represent the difference between a player who follows the priority order and a player who follows the priority order correctly. Applied consistently, they can compress your research roadmap by weeks or months.

The Production Set Protocol

Before queuing any research — especially long overnight timers — always switch to your Production Talent Set and activate the Fast Build talent. This talent reduces the resource cost of the next research you initiate within its activation window. The research time is the same, but the resource cost is lower, meaning you conserve more resources for troop training and future research acceleration. This is a free saving on every single research node and takes less than 30 seconds to execute.

Efficiency Tip: At VIP 10 and above, you have access to the free Talent Memory reset. Use this to switch to your Production Talent Set while research is running, activate Fast Build before queuing your next research, then switch back to your Balance or Attack set. This takes under 60 seconds and saves resources on every node you unlock.

Queue Strategy for Offline Hours

Research timers continue running while you are offline — but only the single currently-queued research (or two if you have a second queue slot unlocked). This makes offline hours your most valuable research time. Always queue your single longest available research before logging off for the night. A 14-hour research running overnight is 14 hours of progress without requiring your presence. A 10-minute research that finishes while you sleep is 13 hours and 50 minutes of wasted queue time.

Alliance Technology Speedups

Your alliance periodically offers research speedup gifts through Alliance Technology contributions. These speedups are often underused by mid-level players who save them indefinitely "for something important." The correct protocol: use research speedups on your longest current research node. The absolute value of a speedup (in hours removed) is constant, but its relative value is highest when applied to the longest timers. Apply speedups the moment they are available rather than hoarding them.

Research Speed Nodes Are Your Highest ROI Investment

This bears repeating because players consistently underweight it. The Research Speed Boost nodes in the Development Tree are the highest return-on-investment nodes in the entire game. Not the highest Research ROI — the highest game-wide ROI. Every percentage point of research speed you gain reduces the timer on every single research node that comes after it. At 50 remaining research nodes and a 10% speed buff, you are saving the equivalent of 5 entire nodes worth of time. At 100 remaining nodes, you saved 10 nodes. Research Speed pays compounding interest for the rest of your account's life.

Second Queue Slot Priority

If your HQ level or VIP status grants access to a second research queue slot, treat it as a permanent resource and fill it at all times. The second slot effectively doubles your research throughput. Use the second slot for shorter Development Tree cleanup nodes while the primary slot runs your long Military Tree priorities. Never leave both slots empty simultaneously.

Advanced Tip: During alliance research events, all research generates alliance contribution points. Time your major research unlocks to coincide with active alliance research events so your progress contributes to event milestones and earns bonus rewards on top of your normal research completion benefits.

What NOT to Research First

Understanding what to research is valuable. Understanding what not to research — and why — is equally important, because misprioritized research cannot be undone and its cost in time is measured in weeks and months, not hours.

Mistake #1 — Secondary Troop Type Military Nodes: Do NOT research secondary troop type military nodes until your primary type's Attack and Health are completely maxed to the highest tier available at your HQ level. Every resource and speedup invested in a secondary troop type while your primary remains incomplete is a direct reduction in your combat effectiveness at your primary role. You become mediocre at everything instead of dominant in one lane.
Mistake #2 — Trap and Wall Defense Nodes: Do NOT research trap and wall defense nodes during your early or mid-game development. These nodes provide negligible practical benefit against experienced attackers who will simply not attack a well-shielded base, and they provide zero offensive capability. The opportunity cost of researching these nodes instead of Military Attack and Health nodes is enormous.
Mistake #3 — Rushing Military Tree While Skipping Development: Do NOT skip the Development Tree to rush the Military Tree. This is the most common and most damaging mistake new players make. The compounding speed benefits of Development Tree research speed nodes are worth the equivalent of 2–3 months of research time over the lifetime of your account. Players who skip this phase spend the rest of their time paying the compounding interest on that mistake.
Mistake #4 — Early Economy Production Nodes: Do NOT research Food Production, Stone Production, or other resource generation nodes early in your development. In-castle resource generation is significantly less efficient than gathering from the map. A single gathering march to a high-tier resource tile returns more in one trip than 24 hours of in-castle production. These economy nodes have low ROI until late game when your gathering efficiency is already maximized and you need passive income supplements.

The Opportunity Cost Framework

Every research node takes time and resources. The opportunity cost of any node is what you could have researched instead. Before starting any research, ask: "Is this node more valuable than the next node in my primary priority order?" If the answer is no, defer it. Your priority order as defined in this guide is already optimized — deviations from it cost you more than they benefit you.

Guideline: If you find yourself researching a node that is not in the Development Phase 1–2 list, the primary troop type Military list, or required as a prerequisite for those lists, stop and reconsider. You are almost certainly off the optimal path.

Long-Term Research Roadmap

The following roadmap represents the optimal research timeline for a daily-active player using the Production Talent Set switching protocol and claiming all standard alliance speedup bonuses. Treat this as a planning framework, not a rigid schedule — your actual timeline will vary based on VIP level, speedup reserves, and HQ progression speed.

Month 1–2: Development Foundation (Phase 1 and Phase 2)

Your entire first two months should be dedicated to completing Development Tree Phase 1 and Phase 2 as defined earlier in this guide. By the end of Month 2, you should have:

  • Advanced Construction unlocked and active.
  • Building Speed Boost I, II, III completed.
  • Research Speed Boost I, II completed — both are mandatory before Month 2 ends.
  • Training Speed I and Training Cost Reduction I, II completed.
  • Gathering Speed, Load Increase, March Speed, and Training Capacity completed.
Milestone Check: At the end of Month 2, if you have not completed Research Speed Boost I and II, you are behind schedule. Prioritize these immediately even if it means deferring other Phase 2 nodes temporarily.

Month 3–6: Military Primary — Attack and Health

Beginning in Month 3, pivot completely to your chosen primary troop type Military Tree. Your sole focus during this period is maxing Attack Boost I through V and Health Boost I through V for your primary type. These ten nodes are your core combat identity and they take the longest. The research speed you built in Phase 1 significantly reduces these timers compared to a player who skipped speed nodes.

By end of Month 6, your primary troop type's Attack and Health should be fully maxed. You are now a genuinely dangerous opponent in your chosen troop lane for the first time. Your alliance will notice the difference in rally performance and defensive holding capacity.

Month 6–12: Military Primary — Defense, Size, and Cleanup

Months 6 through 12 are devoted to completing your primary troop type Military Tree. Research Defense I through III, the troop-specific size increase, and Casualty Reduction nodes. Simultaneously, continue clearing any Development Tree prerequisite nodes required to access higher Military Tree tiers.

By end of Month 12, a daily-active F2P player should have their entire primary troop Military Tree completed to the maximum tier available at their HQ level. This represents a major power threshold — you are now in the top tier of non-spending players in your server bracket.

Month 12+: Secondary Troop and Special Trees

After Month 12, branch into your secondary troop type Military Tree following the same Attack → Health → Defense → Size → Casualty priority order. Simultaneously begin Dragon Research and any Special Research trees that become available as your HQ reaches its higher tiers. These special trees provide unique buffs not available in the core Development or Military trees and represent the final layer of power that separates endgame players from late-game players.

Spender Timeline: Players who invest in research speedups and VIP levels can compress this timeline by 2–3x. A moderate spender following this priority order can complete their primary Military tree by Month 4–5 instead of Month 12. The order of operations does not change — only the speed of execution.

Endgame Research: The Final Percentages

In the endgame — typically Month 18 to 24 for F2P daily players — the remaining research nodes provide smaller incremental buffs but still matter in competitive alliance warfare. At this stage, every percentage point of Attack or Health on 1 million troops is the equivalent of tens of thousands of additional effective troop strength. Do not dismiss late-tree nodes as "marginal gains" — in equal-skill, equal-gear matchups, they are the deciding factor.

The research journey in Puzzles & Conquest never truly ends. New nodes are added with updates, special event trees unlock temporarily, and the Dragon and Artifact research systems provide additional depth for veteran players. Approach research as a continuous practice, not a project with a finish line, and you will consistently outpace the players who treat it as optional.

Final Note: The single most important habit you can build in this game is logging in daily to check your research queue. An empty queue is wasted time. A full queue that you actively manage — queuing the right node, in the right order, with the Production Set switch before each queue — is the foundation of every top-ranking commander on every server.
